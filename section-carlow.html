🇮🇪 About County Carlow

Why Carlow deserves more than a drive-through

"People drive through Carlow on the way to somewhere else and that has always frustrated us. Every single one of those people is passing a county with more stories per square kilometre than almost anywhere else in Ireland. We made these videos to make them pull over."

Carlow is the second smallest county in Ireland and one of the most historically dense. The River Barrow runs through it, the Blackstairs Mountains frame its eastern edge and the county town sits on the site of one of the most strategically important river crossings in medieval Ireland. The castle is in the middle of town. The Dolmen is bigger than most monuments that attract thousands of visitors. The museum punches well above its weight.

And then there are the people. Walt Disney's family is buried here. Myles Keogh — who rode with Custer — is from Leighlinbridge. St Willibrord, who became the patron saint of the Netherlands and Luxembourg and gave Echternach its name, came from this county. Kevin Barry, executed in 1920 at the age of eighteen, had his last cigarette in Carlow. Lucinda Sly terrorised the county in the 18th century with a story that would make a brilliant film. Scientists who changed the world grew up here and are barely remembered.

Walt Disney connection

Disney's ancestors on his father's side were from Carlow — and members of the family are buried in the county. One of the more extraordinary local historical connections in Ireland.

⚔️
Myles Keogh

Born in Leighlinbridge, served in the Papal Army in Rome, fought in the American Civil War and died at the Little Bighorn in 1876. One of the most remarkable and most overlooked Irishmen in history.

⛪
St Willibrord

Born in Carlow in the 7th century, became the first Bishop of Utrecht and patron saint of the Netherlands and Luxembourg. The town of Echternach in Luxembourg still bears his influence.

🌿
Altamont Gardens

One of the most beautiful gardens in Ireland — and known to very few people outside the county. A National Garden open to the public and genuinely extraordinary in any season.

🏛️
Carlow County Museum

Housed in the old town hall, this museum punches well above its weight — a brilliant collection of local history, archaeology and stories that most visitors to Ireland never find.

🗿
Browne's Hill Dolmen

One of the largest portal tombs in Europe — a capstone estimated at 100 tonnes, dating from around 4000 BC — sitting in a field just outside Carlow town with a small car park and almost no visitors.

Common questions

Carlow FAQ

What is Carlow famous for?
Carlow is famous for its rich history — Carlow Castle, Browne's Hill Dolmen (one of the largest portal tombs in Europe), the River Barrow and a remarkable cast of historical figures. Walt Disney's ancestors were buried in Carlow, Myles Keogh — who died at Custer's Last Stand — was from Leighlinbridge, and the notorious 18th-century killer Lucinda Sly operated in the county.
Is Carlow worth visiting?
Absolutely — Carlow is one of the most underrated counties in Ireland. It has extraordinary history, beautiful gardens, the River Barrow, Carlow County Museum, ancient religious sites and a fascinating cast of local characters that most visitors to Ireland never discover. It is also small and walkable, making it an ideal day trip from Dublin or Kilkenny.
Did Walt Disney have Irish ancestors from Carlow?
Yes — Walt Disney's ancestors on his father's side were from County Carlow, and members of the Disney family are buried in the county. It is one of the more extraordinary and least-known historical connections in Ireland.
Who was Myles Keogh?
Myles Keogh was born in Leighlinbridge, County Carlow, served in the Papal Army in Rome, fought in the American Civil War and was killed at the Battle of the Little Bighorn — Custer's Last Stand — in 1876. His horse Comanche was the only US cavalry survivor of the battle. Keogh is one of the most remarkable and most overlooked figures in Irish history.
What are the best things to do in Carlow?
Visit Carlow County Museum, walk to the ruins of Carlow Castle, explore Altamont Gardens (one of the most beautiful in Ireland), see Browne's Hill Dolmen, walk the River Barrow towpath and explore the Cathedral of the Assumption. And watch our 11 short films — they will show you a side of Carlow that no guidebook covers.
Who was Lucinda Sly?
Lucinda Sly was an 18th-century figure associated with a series of crimes in County Carlow — one of the more darkly fascinating stories in the county's history and one that deserves to be far better known. Watch our short film on her story for the full details.
