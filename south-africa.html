🇿🇦 The Story

Twelve days across the most extraordinary country on earth

The Rainbow Nation — beautiful, complex and absolutely unmissable.

"South Africa gave us more emotions in twelve days than most destinations give in a lifetime. The beauty of Cape Town, the raw wilderness of Kruger at dawn, the warmth of the people in Eswatini — and then Soweto and the Apartheid Museum, which left us genuinely speechless. It is a country that demands to be understood, not just visited."

South Africa is one of those destinations that operates on a completely different scale to everything else. The sheer variety of what it offers — world-class cities, ancient wilderness, extraordinary wildlife, deeply moving history and some of the warmest and most resilient people on earth — is unlike anywhere else we have been.

Cape Town set the tone immediately. Arriving to see Table Mountain dominating the skyline is one of those arrival moments that stays with you — and the city more than lives up to it. The Cape Peninsula tour was spectacular: the Cape of Good Hope, the fynbos nature reserve, the penguin colony at Boulders Beach that stopped us in our tracks and the beautiful drive back through Kirstenbosch.

The game drives in Hluhluwe-Imfolozi and Kruger National Park were among the most thrilling experiences of our entire travelling lives. Being in an open vehicle at dawn watching the African bush come alive around you — and then seeing the Big Five in their natural habitat — is genuinely unlike anything else. Kruger in particular is vast and wild and endlessly surprising.

The stop in Eswatini added a rich cultural dimension to the trip — the Ngwenya glass factory, the Matsamo Cultural Village with its traditional dance and song, the beautiful landscape of the kingdom. A perfect complement to the wildlife days on either side of it.

And then Soweto and the Apartheid Museum brought the trip to a close in the most profound way possible. The Apartheid Museum is one of the most important museums we have ever visited — it does not flinch from the truth of South Africa's history and leaves you moved, informed and determined to understand more. The visit to Mandela's former home and Bishop Tutu's former residence in Soweto made history tangible in a way that no book ever quite manages.

South Africa is a country that gives enormously — and asks you to receive it all with open eyes and an open heart. We left changed by it. We would go back tomorrow.

📅 The Itinerary

Day by day — Cape Town to Johannesburg

Twelve days across the Rainbow Nation

DAY1–2
Arrival in Cape Town 🏔️

Flew to Cape Town — undeniably one of the most beautiful cities in the world. Transfer to the hotel with Table Mountain dominating the skyline on arrival. Welcome orientation meeting with our Tour Manager.

✨ First sight of Table Mountain — worth the flight alone
DAY3
Cape Town City Tour & Table Mountain 🚡

Half-day city tour past the Castle of Good Hope (1666), City Hall, St George's Cathedral, the Company Gardens and the Malay Quarter. Cable car to the top of Table Mountain for breathtaking 360-degree views over the city, the Atlantic and Robben Island. Evening at the V&A Waterfront and Welcome Dinner with the group.

✨ Table Mountain — every bit as spectacular as it looks
DAY4
Cape Peninsula — Penguins & Cape of Good Hope 🐧

Full day Cape Peninsula Tour — Cape Point, Hout Bay fishing village, the Cape of Good Hope Nature Reserve (baboons, zebras, bonteboks and extraordinary fynbos flora), the penguin colonies at Boulders Beach and a walk through Kirstenbosch National Botanical Gardens.

🐧 Boulders Beach penguins — an absolute highlight
DAY5
Cape Town at Leisure 🍷

Free day in Cape Town. Optional South African Wine Tour through Paarl, Franschhoek and Stellenbosch, optional Great White Shark cage diving at Shark Alley, or the Daily Music Show celebrating Cape cultural heritage through music, storytelling and traditional cuisine.

DAY6
Flight to Durban — St Lucia Estuary Cruise 🐊

Morning flight from Cape Town to Durban, then on to St Lucia for a game viewing cruise on the St Lucia Estuary — home to Nile crocodiles, hippopotami, sea turtles and sharks. A uniquely diverse and fascinating ecosystem unlike anything else on the trip.

🦛 Hippos in the estuary — extraordinary up close
DAY7
Hluhluwe-Imfolozi Game Reserve & Into Eswatini 🦏

Early morning game drive through Hluhluwe-Imfolozi — famous for its conservation of black and white rhinos and home to Africa's Big Five. Elephant, buffalo, lion, leopard and rhino all possible. Afternoon crossing into the Kingdom of Eswatini with a stop at a traditional handicraft market.

🦏 White rhino at Hluhluwe — a privilege to see
DAY8
Eswatini — Ngwenya Glass & Matsamo Village 🇸🇿

Morning visit to the Ngwenya glass factory — watching talented glassblowers create African animals from recycled glass. Transfer to Matsamo Cultural Village for traditional Swati dance and song, authentic instruments and cuisine, and the opportunity to wander through the village and meet local people.

🎶 Matsamo Cultural Village — a genuinely moving experience
DAY9
Kruger National Park — Full Day Game Drive 🦁

Dawn departure for a full day open-vehicle game drive through Kruger National Park — larger than the state of Connecticut and offering some of the best game viewing in the world. Lion, elephant, giraffe, zebra, cheetah and leopard all possible. One of the greatest wildlife experiences on earth.

🦁 The Big Five in an open vehicle at dawn — nothing like it
DAY10
The Panorama Route to Johannesburg 🌅

Spectacular drive through Mpumalanga — the 16-mile Blyde River Canyon cut into red sandstone, God's Window overlooking Kruger National Park, and Bourke's Luck Potholes with their extraordinary water-carved rock formations. Then on to Johannesburg.

🌅 Blyde River Canyon — one of Africa's great natural wonders
DAY11
Soweto & The Apartheid Museum 🕊️

Half-day Soweto tour — the most metropolitan township in the country, setting trends in politics, music and culture. Nelson Mandela's former home, Bishop Desmond Tutu's former residence, and the deeply powerful Apartheid Museum — one of the most important museums we have ever visited. Farewell Dinner with the group.

🕊️ The Apartheid Museum — essential, moving, unforgettable
DAY12
Departure from Johannesburg ✈️

Transfer to OR Tambo International Airport for the departure flight. Left South Africa with full hearts, extraordinary memories and a deep respect for one of the world's most remarkable countries.

Common questions

South Africa FAQ

Is a South Africa group tour worth it?
Absolutely — South Africa is one of those destinations where a guided group tour genuinely transforms the experience. The distances involved, the game drives, the logistics of moving between Cape Town, KwaZulu-Natal, Eswatini and Johannesburg are all handled for you, leaving you free to focus on the extraordinary experiences at every stop.
Is Cape Town worth visiting?
Cape Town is undeniably one of the most beautiful cities in the world — Table Mountain rising above the city, the V&A Waterfront, the Cape Peninsula's dramatic coastline and the incredible food and wine culture of the surrounding region. It is a city that earns every superlative thrown at it.
Is Kruger National Park worth visiting?
Yes — Kruger National Park is one of the greatest wildlife experiences on earth. Larger than the state of Connecticut, it offers some of the best game viewing in Africa. An open-vehicle game drive at dawn is one of those travel experiences that genuinely has no equal.
What is Table Mountain like?
Table Mountain is every bit as spectacular as it looks — the cable car ride to the top gives you breathtaking 360-degree views over Cape Town, the Atlantic, the Cape Peninsula and, on a clear day, Robben Island. The summit itself has its own unique ecosystem of wildflowers and wildlife.
What is the Apartheid Museum like?
The Apartheid Museum in Johannesburg is one of the most powerful and important museums we have ever visited. It tells the story of racial segregation in South Africa with extraordinary honesty, depth and dignity. It is not an easy visit emotionally — but it is an absolutely essential one.
What is the Panorama Route in South Africa?
The Panorama Route in Mpumalanga is one of the great scenic drives in Africa — the 16-mile Blyde River Canyon, God's Window overlooking Kruger National Park, and Bourke's Luck Potholes with its extraordinary water-carved rock formations. A breathtaking day of natural scenery.
