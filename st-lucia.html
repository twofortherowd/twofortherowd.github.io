St Lucia —
jazz, rum punch and the most dramatic island in the Caribbean

I hopped over to St Lucia for a weekend from Martinique — just 37 kilometres south but a world of its own. 🎷 The Story

A weekend that became one of my best Caribbean memories

"I was living in Martinique at the time and St Lucia was just a short hop south — but it felt like a completely different world. The Jazz Festival was in full swing, the island was electric, and then Steven Seagal walked on stage. Not what you expect at a Caribbean jazz festival. Absolutely brilliant."

When you're living in Martinique, St Lucia is the obvious weekend escape — close enough to reach easily, different enough to feel like a proper adventure. I went during the St Lucia Jazz Festival and the timing could not have been better. The whole island takes on a different energy during festival week — there is music everywhere, the bars and restaurants around Rodney Bay are packed, and the outdoor concert stages in the tropical heat are genuinely extraordinary.

The festival has always mixed genres beautifully — jazz, blues, reggae, soul and crossover celebrity acts — and during my visit the celebrity slot went to none other than Steven Seagal, who was performing with his blues band. Not what you necessarily expect from the man who made his name throwing people through windows on screen, but genuinely brilliant and completely memorable. That alone made the weekend unforgettable.

Beyond the festival, St Lucia is one of the most beautiful and dramatic islands in the entire Caribbean. The Pitons rising from the sea, the lush green hills, the beaches, the rum — it is a place that gets under your skin very quickly.

🎷 The Jazz Festival

The St Lucia Jazz Festival — music, heat and rum punch

The St Lucia Jazz Festival is one of the great Caribbean music events — a week-long celebration of live music that draws world-class acts to outdoor stages across the island. The main concert venue during my visit was an enormous open-air stage with the tropical sky overhead, the warm night air thick with humidity and the smell of food stalls, and thousands of people dancing and swaying to the music.

The festival has always been brilliant at mixing genres — it is called the Jazz Festival but it has never been strictly jazz, and that is very much part of its appeal.

🎷 Jazz 🎸 Blues 🥁 Reggae 🎵 Soul ⭐ Celebrity crossover acts

And then Steven Seagal took the stage with his blues band. The crowd absolutely loved it — he plays with genuine skill and enthusiasm, and the novelty of watching one of Hollywood's most famous action stars performing blues guitar on a Caribbean island under the stars is something you cannot make up. It is the kind of moment that makes travel endlessly surprising.

The food stalls around the festival grounds were brilliant — local St Lucian food, fresh seafood, grilled meats, saltfish accras and tropical fruit. And rum punch. Always rum punch. The St Lucians make it well and they make it strong, and during festival week there is no shortage of opportunities to test this.

🎷 World-class live music ⭐ Steven Seagal blues band 🍹 Rum punch 🍢 Local food stalls 💃 Dancing in tropical heat 🌙 Open-air evening concerts
🎉 Gros Islet Street Party

The Gros Islet Friday Night Street Party

The Gros Islet Friday Night Street Party is one of St Lucia's best-known and most beloved nightlife experiences — and during Jazz Festival week it reaches another level entirely. The small fishing village of Gros Islet, just north of Rodney Bay, transforms completely on Friday nights into one big outdoor party.

The streets are closed to traffic. Street BBQs appear on every corner — chicken, fish, corn, anything you can cook over charcoal in a warm Caribbean night. Rum shacks and improvised bars spring up everywhere. DJs and live music spill out of every doorway. Locals and tourists dance together in the street. There is a brilliant, genuine energy to it — this is not put on for visitors, it is simply what Gros Islet does on a Friday night, and has done for decades.

🍗 Street BBQs 🎵 Loud music everywhere 💃 Dancing in the street 🥃 Rum shacks 🎧 DJs 🤝 Locals and tourists together
"The Gros Islet street party is one of those authentic Caribbean experiences you couldn't plan or manufacture. It just happens — every Friday — and it is brilliant every time."
🌴 Rodney Bay

Rodney Bay — the heart of the festival and the best base on the island

Rodney Bay is the natural home base for the Jazz Festival — and for most visitors to St Lucia. The marina is beautiful, lined with restaurants, bars and live music venues that stay busy well into the night during festival week. The atmosphere around the marina in the evenings — cocktail in hand, music drifting across the water, the warm Caribbean night — is genuinely magical.

Reduit Beach, just a short walk from the marina, is one of the finest beaches on the island — a long, golden stretch of sand with calm turquoise water, beach bars and the easy, relaxed energy that St Lucia does so well. It is the kind of beach where you intend to spend an hour and end up spending the whole afternoon.

⚓ Rodney Bay Marina 🍹 Cocktails by the water 🎵 Live music venues 🏖️ Reduit Beach 🍽️ Marina restaurants 🌅 Evening atmosphere
⛰️ The Pitons

The Pitons — the most dramatic sight in the Caribbean

Even if you spend your entire St Lucia visit in Rodney Bay and Gros Islet, the Pitons are impossible to ignore — they define the island's identity and they are genuinely extraordinary. The two volcanic spires — Gros Piton (798 metres) and Petit Piton (743 metres) — rise almost vertically from the sea on the southwest coast near Soufrière, and they are unlike anything else in the Caribbean.

The Pitons are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and they earn that designation completely. Whether you see them from the water on a boat trip along the coast, from a viewpoint above Soufrière, from a helicopter above the island, or simply as a backdrop driving through the hills — the impact is extraordinary every single time. They are the defining image of St Lucia and one of the most dramatic natural sights in the entire region.

A boat trip from Rodney Bay down the west coast to the Pitons, with stops for snorkelling, a visit to the drive-in volcano at Soufrière and the Diamond Botanical Gardens, is one of the great St Lucia experiences and absolutely worth doing if you have a full day on the island.

⛰️ Gros Piton & Petit Piton 🚤 Boat trip along the coast 🌋 Drive-in volcano, Soufrière 🐠 Snorkelling stops 🌺 Diamond Botanical Gardens 📸 Viewpoints above Soufrière

Common questions

St Lucia FAQ

Is the St Lucia Jazz Festival worth attending?
Absolutely — the St Lucia Jazz Festival is one of the great music events in the Caribbean. The combination of world-class acts, huge outdoor stages in tropical heat, local food stalls, rum punch and an incredible atmosphere makes it unlike any festival we have ever attended. The mix of jazz, blues, reggae, soul and crossover celebrity acts means there is something for everyone.
What is the Gros Islet Friday Night Street Party like?
The Gros Islet Friday Night Street Party is one of St Lucia's best-known nightlife experiences — street BBQs, loud music, dancing in the street, rum shacks, DJs and a brilliant mix of locals and tourists all celebrating together. It is lively, cheap, fun and completely authentic. One of those experiences that gives you a real feel for St Lucian life.
Are the Pitons worth visiting in St Lucia?
The Pitons are one of the most iconic and dramatic natural landmarks in the entire Caribbean — two volcanic spires rising straight from the sea on the southwest coast. They are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and genuinely jaw-dropping in person, whether you see them from the water on a boat trip, from a viewpoint above Soufrière or from the air. They are the defining image of St Lucia for good reason.
Is Rodney Bay a good base in St Lucia?
Rodney Bay is the best base for most visitors to St Lucia — especially during the Jazz Festival. The marina is surrounded by restaurants, bars and live music venues. Reduit Beach is one of the finest beaches on the island. Gros Islet is just up the road for the Friday Night Street Party. It has the best range of accommodation and the liveliest nightlife on the island.
Can you visit St Lucia from Martinique?
Yes — St Lucia is just 37km south of Martinique and very easy to reach by ferry or short flight. When living in Martinique, a weekend trip to St Lucia is one of the most natural and rewarding things you can do. The contrast between the two islands — both beautiful, both Caribbean, but with very different characters — is fascinating.
What is the best time to visit St Lucia?
The dry season from December to April is generally considered the best time to visit — less rain, lower humidity and the finest beach weather. The Jazz Festival traditionally takes place in May, which sits just at the edge of the dry season — warm, occasionally showery but absolutely worth it for the atmosphere. Avoid September and October at the peak of hurricane season.
