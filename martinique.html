🇲🇶 The Story

A year in the Caribbean

What a life. What an island.

"Not many people get to call the Caribbean home — even for a year. But that is exactly what Martinique was. Sun, sand, sea, tropical storms, mosquitoes and some of the most wonderful people I have ever met in my life."

Martinique is one of those places that gets under your skin in the best possible way. From 2006 to 2007, Nicola lived and worked on the island, teaching in two local high schools — which meant experiencing Martinique the way very few visitors ever do. Not as a tourist ticking off the highlights, but as someone embedded in daily life, getting to know the community, the culture and the rhythms of the island properly.

The people are what stand out most. Warm, generous, laid-back and endlessly welcoming — the kind of community that makes you feel at home almost immediately, even when you are a long way from Ireland and everything familiar.

Martinique is not a big tourist destination in the way some Caribbean islands are, and that is genuinely part of its charm. It has its own identity — a fascinating mix of French and Caribbean culture, incredible food, beautiful beaches and landscapes that go from white sand coastline to lush volcanic rainforest within the space of a short drive.

The tropical storms are real, the mosquitoes are very real, and the heat takes some getting used to. But once you settle in, it is the kind of place that is genuinely hard to leave.

Fort-de-France

The vibrant capital — colourful, busy and full of life. Great food, lively bars and a real French Caribbean energy that makes it unlike any other city.

The Beaches

From the famous white sands of Les Salines to quieter coves around the coast — the beaches here are genuinely beautiful and rarely overcrowded.

The north of the island is lush, green and dramatic — proper tropical rainforest with hiking trails, waterfalls and views that take your breath away.

🌋
Mount Pelée

One of the most dramatic volcanoes in the Caribbean — the hike to the top rewards you with spectacular views across the island on a clear day.

🍽️
The Food

A brilliant fusion of French technique and Caribbean ingredients. Accras de morue, colombo, fresh seafood and the best rum punch you will ever have.

🤝
The People

Without question the highlight of the whole experience. Warm, friendly, laid-back and genuinely welcoming — the community here is something really special.

Common questions

Martinique FAQ

Is Martinique a good holiday destination?
Absolutely. Martinique is a stunning Caribbean island with beautiful beaches, lush rainforest, great food and a real French Caribbean culture that makes it feel unlike anywhere else. It is not a typical mass-tourism destination, which is actually part of what makes it so special — it feels authentic and unhurried in a way that some better-known Caribbean islands have lost.
What is Martinique like to live in?
Living in Martinique is an extraordinary experience. The pace of life is relaxed, the people are incredibly warm and friendly, and you are surrounded by natural beauty every single day. There are tropical storms to contend with and mosquitoes are very much part of daily life, but the sunshine, the beaches and the community more than make up for it. Nicola spent a full year here teaching and would go back in a heartbeat.
What is Fort-de-France like?
Fort-de-France is the vibrant capital of Martinique — a busy, colourful city with a great mix of French and Caribbean culture. There are excellent restaurants, lively bars and a real energy about the place. It is worth spending proper time here rather than just passing through — the food scene alone is worth it.
What is the best time of year to visit Martinique?
The dry season from December to May is generally considered the best time to visit, with lower humidity and less chance of tropical storms. That said, Martinique is warm and beautiful year-round — just be aware that the hurricane season runs from June to November, which can bring heavy rain and the occasional proper storm. Having lived through a few, they are an experience in themselves!
Is Martinique safe for tourists?
Martinique is generally a safe destination for tourists. Like anywhere, it pays to be sensible and aware of your surroundings, particularly in busier urban areas. The island is an overseas region of France so standards of infrastructure and services are good, and the locals are genuinely welcoming to visitors.
Do you need to speak French to visit Martinique?
French is the official language and Antillean Creole is also widely spoken. English is not as common here as in some Caribbean destinations, so a few basic French phrases will go a long way and will be genuinely appreciated by locals. That said, people are friendly and patient — you will get by fine even with limited French, and making the effort is always warmly received.
