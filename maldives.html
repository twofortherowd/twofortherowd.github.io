🏝️ The Trip

The honeymoon finale

Ten nights in the most beautiful place on earth.

"After Zanzibar and Kenya, we thought nothing could top what we had already experienced on our honeymoon. Then we arrived in the Maldives. We were wrong."

After three extraordinary weeks — starting in Zanzibar and then heading on safari in Kenya — we arrived on Meeru Island in the Maldives for the final chapter of our honeymoon. Ten nights. All inclusive. An overwater villa. And the Indian Ocean in every direction as far as the eye could see.

The pace of life on Meeru Island is exactly what you need after a busy few weeks of travel. Nicola threw herself into the scuba diving — the marine life in the Maldives is extraordinary and she dived throughout the stay. Podge lay in the sun, played golf, boules and pool, and made very enthusiastic use of the all inclusive drinks policy.

Every evening we sat out together listening to the live bands play as the sun went down over the ocean — and the sunsets in the Maldives are genuinely among the most beautiful things we have ever seen. We did a catamaran tour, had a couples massage and swam out from the beach one afternoon to find ourselves surrounded by baby sharks in the shallows. Completely harmless — and completely breathtaking.

The romantic highlights were something else. A private dinner on the beach, the table surrounded by a love heart made of flowers and candles, the ocean lapping nearby — it was one of the most special evenings of our lives. And when Nicola's birthday came around, the resort staff surprised her with a beautifully made gluten-free birthday cake. A genuinely lovely touch.

Now — the other story. Podge joined a football match against the resort staff, took a knock and ended up at the island doctor. Recovered. Then got a dodgy stomach and ended up back at the island doctor a second time. Throughout all of this, Nicola was absolutely fine and continued sipping cocktails with impressive dedication. The staff were brilliant throughout — warm, professional and endlessly good-humoured about the whole thing.

Ten nights was not enough. We would go back tomorrow.

Common questions

Maldives FAQ

Is the Maldives worth it for a honeymoon?
Absolutely — the Maldives is one of the world's ultimate honeymoon destinations and completely lives up to its reputation. The overwater villas, the turquoise lagoons, the incredible marine life and the pure relaxation make it unlike anywhere else on earth. If you can go, go.
Is Meeru Island a good resort in the Maldives?
Yes — Meeru Island is a brilliant resort with a fantastic range of activities, excellent food across multiple restaurants, a great all-inclusive offering and wonderfully friendly staff. It is one of the larger Maldivian islands which means there is genuinely plenty to do beyond just lying on the beach — though that is also excellent.
Is all inclusive worth it in the Maldives?
Yes — given how expensive drinks and food can be in the Maldives, all inclusive is absolutely worth it. It takes all the stress out of budgeting while you are there and means you can fully relax and enjoy everything the resort has to offer without worrying about the bill.
Can you see sharks in the Maldives?
Yes — and it is one of the most extraordinary experiences the Maldives has to offer. We swam out from the beach and found ourselves surrounded by baby sharks in the shallows — completely harmless and an absolutely unforgettable moment. The marine life in the Maldives is extraordinary and snorkelling and diving here is world class.
Is scuba diving good in the Maldives?
The Maldives is one of the best scuba diving destinations in the world — the visibility, the marine life and the coral are all extraordinary. Nicola dived throughout our stay and absolutely loved every session. Whether you are an experienced diver or a complete beginner, the Maldives is a brilliant place to dive.
Is the Maldives good for gluten free?
On an all-inclusive resort like Meeru Island, GF options are well catered for — the staff go out of their way to accommodate dietary requirements. They even baked a gluten-free birthday cake which was a wonderful surprise. It is always worth flagging your requirements when you book and confirming on arrival.
