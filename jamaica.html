✍️ Our Jamaica Story

Jamaica: More Than Just a Caribbean Holiday

From the moment we arrived, we discovered an island rich in culture, history, natural beauty and some of the friendliest people we've met on our travels. During our stay, we explored well beyond our resort in Montego Bay — and that made all the difference.

One of our favourite days was a trip to Negril, where we enjoyed the famous Seven Mile Beach and watched the sunset from the iconic cliffs. If you've never seen a Caribbean sunset from a clifftop bar with a cocktail in your hand while cliff divers launch themselves into the water below — add it to the list immediately.

We also visited the legendary Rose Hall Great House, one of Jamaica's most famous historic attractions. The beautifully restored plantation house offers a fascinating insight into the island's colonial past and is steeped in local folklore and mystery — particularly the legend of the White Witch of Rose Hall. It's the kind of place that stays with you.

No visit to Jamaica would be complete without learning about its most famous son, so another highlight was our journey to Nine Mile, the birthplace and final resting place of Bob Marley. Travelling through the island's mountainous interior gave us a glimpse of rural Jamaican life, with spectacular scenery, small communities and breathtaking views along the way. The site itself is genuinely moving, regardless of how much of a Marley fan you are.

Most of our excursions were booked through GetYourGuide, which made it easy to organise day trips and explore different parts of the island. From historical sites and cultural experiences to scenic tours and local attractions, it gave us the opportunity to experience much more of Jamaica than we would have seen by staying solely within the resort.

One of the things that struck us most was the warmth and resilience of the Jamaican people. Whether chatting with local guides, drivers, hotel staff or people we met along the way, there was a genuine sense of pride in their island and a willingness to share their culture, stories and traditions.

Of course, Jamaica's natural beauty is impossible to ignore. Crystal-clear Caribbean waters, swaying palm trees and spectacular sunsets create the postcard-perfect image that attracts visitors from around the world. Yet the island's lush mountains, tropical vegetation and hidden corners are equally impressive.

Food lovers will also find plenty to enjoy. From traditional jerk chicken and fresh seafood to tropical fruits and local specialities, Jamaican cuisine is packed with flavour and reflects the island's rich blend of cultural influences. We ate extremely well.

Our time in Jamaica was a wonderful combination of relaxation, adventure and cultural discovery. Whether exploring Montego Bay, discovering Negril, visiting Rose Hall Great House, paying tribute to Bob Marley in Nine Mile or simply enjoying the Caribbean sunshine, every day brought something new.

Jamaica is much more than a holiday destination. It's a place with a rich identity, fascinating history and a spirit that stays with you long after you've returned home.

Would we return? Without a doubt.

One Love, Jamaica. 🇯🇲

⭐ Highlights of Our Trip

The moments that made it

🏖️
Seven Mile Beach, Negril

One of the Caribbean's great beaches — long, calm, beautiful water and a relaxed atmosphere that's hard to leave. The sunset from the cliffs at Rick's Café nearby is unmissable.

🏚️
Rose Hall Great House

A beautifully restored plantation house with one of Jamaica's most compelling ghost stories attached. The legend of the White Witch Annie Palmer is genuinely fascinating.

🎵
Nine Mile — Bob Marley's Birthplace

The journey through the mountains is as good as the destination. Nine Mile is genuinely moving — the house where Marley grew up, his mausoleum and the views over the Jamaican interior.

🚣
Bamboo Rafting on the Lethe River

One of the most relaxing things we did all trip — drifting downstream through tropical vegetation on a bamboo raft. Peaceful, beautiful and genuinely unique to Jamaica.

🍗
The Food

Jerk chicken, fresh seafood, tropical fruits, rum punch — Jamaican food is seriously underrated. Eat wherever the locals eat and you will not go wrong.

🤝
The People

Without question the thing that stayed with us most. The warmth, the pride, the humour and the generosity of the Jamaican people made every day on the island better.

❓ Jamaica FAQ

Common questions about Jamaica

Is Jamaica worth visiting?
Absolutely — Jamaica is much more than the fly-and-flop Caribbean holiday it's often marketed as. The island has an extraordinary culture, history, music scene and natural beauty that goes well beyond the resort. Nine Mile, Rose Hall Great House, Negril's cliffs and the island's lush mountainous interior are all well worth exploring.
What are the best things to do in Jamaica?
Some of the best things to do in Jamaica include visiting Bob Marley's birthplace at Nine Mile, watching the sunset from Rick's Café at Negril, touring Rose Hall Great House, bamboo rafting on the Lethe River and spending time on Seven Mile Beach. Book excursions through GetYourGuide for the easiest experience.
Is Montego Bay a good base for Jamaica?
Yes — Montego Bay makes an excellent base. It has a good range of resorts and is well positioned for day trips to Negril, Nine Mile, Rose Hall and other attractions. Most of the island's main sights are reachable as day trips from MoBay.

What is Rose Hall Great House?
Rose Hall Great House is one of Jamaica's most famous historic attractions near Montego Bay. The beautifully restored plantation house offers a fascinating insight into the island's colonial past and is steeped in local folklore — particularly the legend of the White Witch of Rose Hall, Annie Palmer. Genuinely compelling.
Is Nine Mile worth visiting?
Yes — Nine Mile is Bob Marley's birthplace and final resting place in the mountainous interior of Jamaica. The journey through the island's rural landscape is as memorable as the destination itself, and the site is genuinely moving for anyone with even a passing interest in Marley's music and legacy. Don't miss it.
