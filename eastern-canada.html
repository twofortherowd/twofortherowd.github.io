Eastern Canada —
Niagara Falls, the Irish Loop and the trip of a lifetime

In 2009 we embarked on one of our greatest ever road trips — from Toronto all the way to Newfoundland, taking in the cosmopolitan cities, French and British heritage and majestic landscapes of Eastern Canada. We saw traditional Pow Wow dancing in Ottawa, stood in awe at Niagara Falls, explored the gorgeous islands of Prince Edward Island and Nova Scotia, and finished in Newfoundland — where the Irish accents stopped us in our tracks and the Irish Loop gave us one of the most unexpected and moving travel experiences of our lives. Canada is vast, extraordinary and genuinely unforgettable. Eastern Canada proved that beyond any doubt.

🍁 The Trip

Toronto to Newfoundland — an unforgettable journey

Across one of the world's greatest countries — and closer to home than we expected.

"We were not prepared for Newfoundland. We knew intellectually about the Irish heritage — but nothing prepares you for stepping off the ferry, opening your mouth to ask for directions and hearing your own accent come straight back at you. It was one of the most extraordinary moments of any trip we have ever done."

Eastern Canada in 2009 was one of the great adventures of our travelling lives. We started in Toronto — one of the most vibrant and cosmopolitan cities in North America, where French and English culture collide in the most wonderful way. The food, the music, the architecture and the sheer energy of the place set the tone for everything that followed.

Ottawa was a revelation. Canada's capital is often overshadowed by Montréal and Toronto but it is a genuinely brilliant city — the Parliament Buildings are stunning, the museums are world class and watching traditional Pow Wow dancing was one of those travel experiences that stays with you long after you've left. Indigenous culture and heritage is woven into the fabric of Ottawa in a way that is both moving and illuminating.

Niagara Falls needs no introduction — but it still managed to exceed our expectations. No photograph, no video, nothing prepares you for the sheer thundering volume of water going over those falls. You feel it as much as you see it. It is one of the great natural spectacles on earth and we loved every minute of it.

Prince Edward Island and Nova Scotia gave us a completely different side of Canada — quieter, more intimate, breathtakingly beautiful in a gentler way. PEI's red-soiled farmland and stunning beaches, Nova Scotia's dramatic Atlantic coastline and seafood culture — both extraordinary in their own right.

And then Newfoundland. We had heard about the Irish connection but nothing fully prepared us for it. The accents, the place names, the music, the warmth of the people — it felt like Ireland had been transplanted to the edge of the North Atlantic. Driving the Irish Loop on the Avalon Peninsula was one of the most unexpectedly emotional travel experiences we have ever had. A country within a country, carrying a heritage that has never been forgotten.

📍 The Destinations

Six incredible stops — each one its own story

🏙️
Montréal

Canada's cultural heartbeat — French charm, world-class food, iconic architecture and a nightlife scene that rivals anywhere in North America. The perfect starting point for any Eastern Canada adventure.

🥁
Ottawa

The underrated capital. Stunning Parliament Buildings, excellent museums, beautiful Rideau Canal — and the highlight of traditional Pow Wow dancing that gave us one of the most memorable cultural experiences of the whole trip.

💦
Niagara Falls

One of the great natural wonders of the world — and it absolutely delivers. The thundering, cascading water, the spray, the noise and the sheer scale of it is something that photographs simply cannot capture.

🏝️
Prince Edward Island

Gorgeous and unexpected — rolling red-soiled farmland, beautiful beaches, charming fishing villages and a gentle pace of life that feels like a different world from the mainland. Utterly lovely.

⚓
Nova Scotia

Dramatic Atlantic coastline, colourful fishing villages, world-famous seafood and a strong Celtic heritage that feels familiar and surprising in equal measure. Halifax is a brilliant city too.

🇮🇪
Newfoundland

The most extraordinary surprise of the whole trip. Irish accents, Irish place names, Irish music — and the Irish Loop on the Avalon Peninsula, one of the most moving and memorable drives we have ever done.

The standout moments

🇮🇪
The Irish Loop, Newfoundland

A coastal driving route on the Avalon Peninsula through communities settled by Irish immigrants centuries ago — and where the accents, place names and culture are still unmistakably Irish. For an Irish visitor, this is extraordinary.

💦
Niagara Falls

One of the most powerful natural spectacles on earth. You feel the spray on your face, hear the roar from a distance and stand there genuinely speechless. Every bit as magnificent as it's supposed to be.

🥁
Pow Wow Dancing, Ottawa

Watching traditional Indigenous Pow Wow dancing in Ottawa was one of the most culturally rich and genuinely moving travel experiences of the whole trip. Something we had never seen before and have never forgotten.

🏙️
Montréal

Cosmopolitan, bilingual, brilliant — Montréal is one of the great North American cities. The Old Town, the food markets, the festivals and the French-Canadian culture make it utterly distinctive.

🏝️
The Atlantic Islands

Prince Edward Island and Nova Scotia are both gorgeous in completely different ways — PEI's red earth and gentle charm, Nova Scotia's rugged Atlantic drama. Both deserve far more attention than they get.

🍁
The Scale of It All

Eastern Canada is vast — the landscapes shift from city to farmland to forest to coastline to Atlantic rock in ways that constantly surprise you. The sheer scale and variety of it all is one of the great things about Canada as a travel destination.

Common questions

Eastern Canada FAQ

Is Eastern Canada worth visiting?
Absolutely — Eastern Canada is one of the great road trip destinations in the world. From the cosmopolitan energy of Montréal to the majestic power of Niagara Falls, the rolling red fields of Prince Edward Island and the rugged Atlantic coastline of Newfoundland, the variety of landscapes, cultures and experiences is extraordinary.
What is the Irish Loop in Newfoundland?
The Irish Loop is a scenic coastal driving route on the Avalon Peninsula in Newfoundland, named for the strong Irish heritage of the communities along the way. The area was heavily settled by Irish immigrants in the 18th and 19th centuries and the accents, place names and culture still reflect that heritage strongly today. For Irish visitors it is a particularly special and moving experience.
Is Niagara Falls worth visiting?
Yes — Niagara Falls is one of those natural wonders that genuinely exceeds expectations in person. The sheer volume of water cascading over the falls, the noise, the spray and the scale of it all is something photographs simply cannot capture. See it from the Canadian side for the best views.
What is Ottawa like?
Ottawa is a brilliant and often underrated capital city — beautiful architecture, excellent museums, a vibrant food scene and a genuinely welcoming atmosphere. The Parliament Buildings are stunning, the Rideau Canal is lovely and the cultural programme including traditional Indigenous events like Pow Wow performances makes it one of the most interesting cities in Canada.
What is Prince Edward Island like?
Prince Edward Island is one of the most beautiful and unexpected places in Canada — a small island province with rolling red-soiled farmland, stunning beaches, charming fishing villages and a pace of life that feels completely different from the mainland. It is also the setting for Anne of Green Gables, which gives it a particular literary and cultural charm.
How long do you need for an Eastern Canada road trip?
To do Eastern Canada properly — Montréal, Ottawa, Niagara Falls, Nova Scotia, Prince Edward Island and Newfoundland — you need at least two to three weeks. The distances are significant and each destination rewards time. A guided tour is a brilliant way to cover more ground without spending your trip in planning mode.
