🇦🇱 The Trip

The story

A ferry, ancient ruins and one of the most beautiful natural sights we've ever seen.

"Albania genuinely took us by surprise. We arrived not knowing quite what to expect and left completely enchanted — by the scenery, the history, the food and above all by the warmth and friendliness of the people."

We were on holiday in Corfu in 2018 when we decided to take the short ferry crossing over to Albania for the day. It was one of the best decisions we made. The ferry from Corfu to Saranda takes around 35 minutes and runs regularly — it could not be easier to add Albania onto a Corfu trip.

We joined a guided tour once we arrived in Saranda, which was a brilliant way to make the most of a day trip. The guides were knowledgeable, enthusiastic and proud to show off their country — and with good reason. The ancient ruins we visited were extraordinary in scale and atmosphere, sitting in a beautiful landscape that felt a world away from the tourist trails of nearby Greece.

The absolute highlight of the day was the Blue Eye — Syri i Kaltër in Albanian. It is a natural spring where fresh water bubbles up from underground with such clarity and intensity that the pool takes on a vivid, electric blue colour that seems almost too beautiful to be real. The setting in the surrounding woodland makes it even more magical. It is one of the most extraordinary natural sights we have ever seen anywhere.

Throughout the day we were struck by how friendly and welcoming everyone we met was. Albania is a country that is increasingly finding its place on the European travel map — and it deserves every bit of the attention it is getting. It is beautiful, fascinating and genuinely special.

Common questions

Albania FAQ

Can you visit Albania as a day trip from Corfu?
Yes — there is a regular ferry service between Corfu and Saranda that makes Albania very accessible as a day trip. The crossing takes around 35 minutes and several operators run multiple sailings a day. It is one of the most unique and rewarding day trips you can do from Corfu.
What is the Blue Eye in Albania?
The Blue Eye (Syri i Kaltër) is a stunning natural spring near Saranda where crystal-clear water bubbles up from an underground river. The water is an extraordinary vivid blue colour and the surrounding woodland setting makes it one of the most beautiful natural sights in Albania.
Is Albania safe to visit?
Yes — Albania is a very welcoming and safe country for tourists. The locals we encountered were incredibly friendly and hospitable. Like anywhere, take normal travel precautions, but we felt very comfortable throughout our visit.
What is Saranda like?
Saranda is a lively coastal town in southern Albania with a beautiful waterfront promenade, clear Ionian Sea views, and easy access to some incredible ancient sites including Butrint — a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is a great base for exploring the Albanian Riviera.
Do you need a visa to visit Albania from Ireland?
Irish and EU citizens do not need a visa to visit Albania. You can enter with a valid passport. It is a straightforward and hassle-free destination for Irish and European travellers.
Is Albania worth visiting?
Absolutely — Albania is a hidden gem that is increasingly on the radar of European travellers, and rightly so. It has stunning natural scenery, incredible ancient history, a beautiful coastline, delicious food and some of the warmest and most welcoming people we have encountered anywhere in Europe.
