Before you book

Things to know before booking a Morocco tour

The honest bits most reviews gloss over.

🚌
Some driving days are long

Morocco is bigger than it looks on a map. The Marrakech to desert leg takes serious time. Worth knowing upfront — and absolutely worth it.

🥾
Good footwear is non-negotiable

The medinas are cobbled, the terrain changes constantly. Comfortable, broken-in shoes are essential. We learned this the hard way.

🌡️
Bring layers for desert evenings

The Sahara gets surprisingly cold after sunset. A warm layer for the desert camp is essential — don't let this catch you out.

🕌
Marrakech is full-on at first

The medina is loud, hectic, and overwhelming when you first arrive. Give yourself an hour to find your feet — then you'll love it.

🌄
The Sahara day is long but magical

Camel trek, sunset dunes, camp dinner, stars — it's tiring in the best possible way. One of those days you never forget.

📵
Mobile signal gets patchy

Especially around the desert areas. Download Google Maps and anything else you need offline before you leave Marrakech.

Common questions

Morocco tour FAQ — the questions we get asked most

Is a Morocco guided tour actually worth it?
Yes — genuinely, and without hesitation. The distances involved and the sheer complexity of getting between Marrakech, the Atlas Mountains, and the Sahara make a guided tour dramatically better value than trying to piece it all together yourself. You see more, stress less, and get access to experiences — local guides, off-road routes, authentic stops — that you'd never find independently. We'd book it again tomorrow.
Is TourRadar reliable for booking Morocco tours?
We used TourRadar for both our multi-day Morocco tour and our Marrakech walking tour, and had zero issues with either booking. It's a well-established platform, the booking process is straightforward, and customer support is responsive. The key is picking the right operator — we went with Morhara Morocco Tours for the main trip and they were excellent. The Marrakech walking tour with Ali from Get Marrakech was equally brilliant.
How many days do you need to see Morocco properly?
To do Morocco justice — including Marrakech, the Atlas Mountains, and the Sahara desert — you need at least 7 to 10 days. Anything shorter and you'll spend too much time in transit. The multi-day tour we did felt like exactly the right pace — enough time to go deep into the experience without exhausting yourself.
Is Marrakech worth visiting on its own?
Absolutely — Marrakech is brilliant and easily worth 2 to 3 full days. The medina, the souks, Jemaa el-Fnaa, the food scene, the architecture — there's loads to explore. But if you only visit Marrakech, you'll miss the very best of Morocco: the Atlas Mountains, the kasbahs, and the Sahara. If time and budget allow, do the full tour.

If you are just doing Marrakech, book a guided walking tour — it's the single thing that makes the biggest difference to how much you get out of the city. Book the Marrakech walking tour we did →
Should you do a guided tour or book Morocco independently?
For a first trip — especially if you want to reach the Sahara — go with a guided tour, no question. The road distances are enormous, the logistics are complicated, and a knowledgeable local guide adds layers of context and access that you simply won't get going solo. If you've already been and want to do your own thing at your own pace, independent travel works well. But first trip? Guided tour every time.
Is Morocco a good destination for gluten-free travellers?
Much better than we expected, honestly. Tagines, slow-cooked lamb and chicken dishes, grilled meats, rice and couscous, fresh salads, roasted vegetables — a lot of traditional Moroccan food is naturally gluten-free. Cross-contamination can be an issue in busier tourist areas, so being clear when ordering is important. We had no issues on our trip. It's one of the better long-haul destinations we've visited from a gluten-free perspective.
What is the Sahara desert camp experience actually like?
One of the best nights of our lives — genuinely. Arriving at the dunes by camel as the sun goes down, having dinner at the camp, sitting around a fire in the middle of the desert, and waking up at sunrise with nothing but sand dunes around you — it's the kind of experience that's very hard to put into words. It's not a luxury resort. It's better than that. The setting does all the work.
Can you fly to Morocco from Ireland?
Yes — Ryanair flies direct from Dublin to Marrakech, which makes it very convenient for Irish travellers. Flight time is roughly 3 to 3.5 hours. It's one of the most straightforward long-haul-feeling destinations you can get to from Ireland without a long journey. Check current routes and prices before booking as schedules change seasonally.
