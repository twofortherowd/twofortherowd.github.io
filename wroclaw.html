🇵🇱 The Trip

The story

The trip that had everything — including a Polish hospital.

"Only on a Two for the Row'd trip could you land on a Friday evening and end up spending the night in a Polish hospital. But that is exactly what happened — and Wrocław still managed to be absolutely magical."

We flew into Wrocław on a Friday evening in December 2025, full of excitement for the Christmas markets. What followed was not quite what we had planned — a friend took a turn in the pub and we found ourselves navigating a Polish hospital for the rest of the night. Not the start we had imagined, but a story we will be telling for years.

The Polish healthcare staff were brilliant — warm, professional and genuinely kind. A reminder that the friendliness of the Wrocław people extends well beyond the city's restaurants and market stalls.

By Saturday afternoon we were out and about, and Wrocław more than made up for the rocky start. The Christmas market on the Rynek is genuinely one of the most beautiful we have ever seen — hundreds of wooden stalls, the smell of mulled wine and roasted chestnuts in the air, and the stunning baroque buildings of the Old Town lit up all around. It is the kind of atmosphere that makes you feel like you have stepped into a Christmas card.

The food was brilliant and remarkably good value — pierogis, hearty Polish soups and excellent drinks that barely dented the wallet. Everything in Wrocław is affordable compared to most Western European cities, which makes it a brilliant destination for a weekend break.

We flew home on Sunday morning with tired legs, warm hearts and one very good story. Wrocław — we will absolutely be back.

🎄
The Christmas Markets

One of the best in Europe — the Rynek market is magical, with hundreds of stalls, mulled wine and the most beautiful backdrop of baroque Old Town buildings lit up at night.

A beautiful boutique hotel right in the Old Town — stylish, warm and perfectly located for exploring the markets and the city on foot.

🥟
The Food

Pierogis, hearty soups, grilled meats and mulled everything — Polish food is warming, delicious and incredibly good value. You will eat brilliantly without spending much.

🤝
The People

Genuinely some of the friendliest we have encountered anywhere — warm, welcoming and helpful in every situation. Even the unexpected ones.

Common questions

Wrocław FAQ

Is Wrocław worth visiting for the Christmas markets?
Absolutely — Wrocław is one of the best Christmas market destinations in Europe. The main market on the Rynek is magical, with hundreds of stalls, mulled wine, traditional food and a festive atmosphere that is hard to beat. The city's beautiful architecture lit up at Christmas makes it even more special.
How cold is Wrocław in December?
Very cold! December temperatures in Wrocław typically hover around 0°C to 3°C and it can feel significantly colder with wind chill. Pack your warmest layers, a heavy coat, thermal underlayers and proper winter boots. The cold is part of the Christmas market experience — but you need to be prepared for it.
Is Wrocław good value for money?
Yes — Wrocław is excellent value compared to most Western European cities. Food, drinks and accommodation are all very affordable, which makes it a brilliant destination for a weekend break. You can eat and drink very well without spending a fortune.
What are the must-see sights in Wrocław?
The Rynek (Market Square) is the heart of the city and one of the most beautiful in Central Europe. St. Elizabeth's Cathedral is stunning inside and out. The famous Wrocław dwarfs — small bronze gnome statues hidden around the city — are brilliant fun to hunt down. And at Christmas, the whole city centre becomes a wonderland.
How many days do you need in Wrocław?
A long weekend of three to four days is ideal — it gives you time to explore the Old Town properly, visit the Cathedral, hunt for the dwarfs, enjoy the Christmas markets at a leisurely pace and sample the food and drink scene. Even a short two-day visit like ours gave us a real taste of what a special city it is.
Are the people in Wrocław friendly?
Incredibly so. We found the people in Wrocław to be some of the warmest and most welcoming we have encountered on our travels — and that extended to some unexpected situations too! English is fairly widely spoken in the city centre, restaurants and hotels, making it a very easy destination for Irish and British visitors.
