Tasmania —
Australia's wild side we didn't expect

Tasmania completely caught us off guard — in the best way possible. We flew from Sydney to Hobart thinking we were heading for "a nice scenic few days"… and ended up in one of the most jaw-droppingly beautiful places we've ever been in 57 countries. Dramatic coastlines, empty roads, unreal national parks, and views that made us stop the car every 10 minutes just to take it all in.

The trip

How it all played out

We landed into Hobart, hopped onto our tour, and basically let Tasmania do its thing. No stress, no overplanning — just pure exploring. And Tasmania absolutely delivered.

First up was Cradle Mountain. Proper wild. Mist rolling in over ancient peaks, lakes that looked completely fake, and that rare quiet you only get in places that feel genuinely untouched. One of those landscapes that makes you feel very small in the best possible way.

Then Russell Falls — straight out of a postcard. One of those spots where you just stand there thinking "how is this real?" The rainforest around it is extraordinary.

And then came the Bay of Fires. Bright orange lichen-covered rocks, white sand, and that ridiculous turquoise water. Genuinely one of the most beautiful coastlines we've ever seen anywhere in the world. The colours don't look real — but they are.

But the one that stayed with us most? Wineglass Bay. The walk up to the lookout, the view of that perfect curve of white sand below, the stillness of it — it's the kind of place that stops you completely. One of the best views in Australia. Possibly one of the best views anywhere.

The highlights

The places that stood out most

🏔️
Cradle Mountain

Raw, wild and completely unforgettable. Mist, ancient rainforest, mirror-still lakes and a silence that feels prehistoric. One of the great natural landscapes of Australia.

🔥
Bay of Fires

Orange lichen-covered rocks plus that impossible turquoise water equals total madness. One of the most photographed coastlines in Australia — and it earns every photo.

🏖️
Wineglass Bay

The view from the lookout over that perfect arc of white sand is genuinely one of the best in Australia. The walk is worth every step — take your time getting up there.

🌿
Freycinet National Park

Unreal coastal landscapes, pink granite peaks, hidden beaches and pristine bush. One of those national parks that reminds you why national parks exist.

💧
Russell Falls

Peaceful, beautiful and completely hidden gem vibes. The walk through the rainforest to get there is as good as the falls themselves.

🦘
The wildlife

Wallabies, wombats, Tasmanian devils, echidnas and more. Tasmania has wildlife you genuinely won't see anywhere else on earth — keep your eyes open constantly.

Common questions

Tasmania FAQ

Is Tasmania worth visiting?
Absolutely — Tasmania is one of the most underrated destinations in the world. The landscapes are extraordinary, the wildlife is unlike anywhere else, and the combination of dramatic coastlines, ancient rainforests and wild national parks makes it genuinely jaw-dropping. We went expecting a nice scenic few days and left completely blown away. In 57 countries between us, it still stands out.
What are the best places to visit in Tasmania?
The highlights are Cradle Mountain, Wineglass Bay, the Bay of Fires, Freycinet National Park and Russell Falls. Wineglass Bay in particular is one of the most beautiful views in Australia — the walk up to the lookout is absolutely worth every step. The Bay of Fires is equally extraordinary — those orange lichen-covered rocks against the turquoise water don't look real until you're standing in front of them.
Is a guided tour of Tasmania worth it?
Yes — a guided tour of Tasmania means you see far more than you would independently, without the stress of navigating remote roads or sorting accommodation in national parks. The distances between the highlights are bigger than they look on a map and a tour handles all of that for you. We booked through TourRadar and it was completely stress-free.
How long do you need in Tasmania?
To see the main highlights — Cradle Mountain, Wineglass Bay, Bay of Fires and Hobart — allow at least five to seven days. Tasmania is bigger than most people expect and rushing it would mean missing the best of it. A week feels just right — enough to go deep without exhausting yourself.
What is the best time of year to visit Tasmania?
December to February is the Australian summer and the most popular time to visit — warmest weather and longest days. But Tasmania has a wild, moody beauty in autumn and spring too. Whatever time of year you go, pack layers and a proper waterproof jacket — the weather can change very quickly. Four seasons in one day is not an exaggeration.

Would we go back?

In a heartbeat. Tasmania is one of those places that sneaks up on you — and then you can't stop thinking about it after. If Australia is on your radar, don't skip Tasmania. It's the best thing we did on the whole trip.
