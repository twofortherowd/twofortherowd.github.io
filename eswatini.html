the kingdom that got deep under our skin

We visited Eswatini — formerly known as Swaziland — in 2018 as part of a wider South Africa trip, and it turned out to be one of the most genuinely moving parts of the entire journey. 🇸🇿 The Story

A small kingdom with a very big heart

The visit that moved us most on our entire southern Africa journey.

"We expected Eswatini to be a stopover. It became one of the most profound travel experiences we have ever had. The children at that school — the joy, the energy, the warmth — it puts everything into perspective. We think about that visit all the time."

We crossed into Eswatini from South Africa in 2018 with one night planned in Mbabane and a loose itinerary for the following day. What unfolded over those two days ended up being among the most memorable of our entire African adventure.

Mbabane is a small capital city set in the beautiful Mdimba Mountains — unhurried, friendly and genuinely charming in a way that big cities rarely are. The markets, the local restaurants, the mountain setting and the warmth of the people we met made an immediate impression.

The visit to the local tribe was extraordinary. Learning about traditional Swazi life — the customs, the crafts, the music, the structure of the community — in a setting that felt completely genuine and unhurried was a rare privilege. These are not performances for tourists. This is real life, shared generously with visitors who take the time to come and learn.

But nothing prepared us for the primary school visit. The children were incredible. The joy, the curiosity, the energy, the warmth they showed to two Irish people turning up in their school — it was one of those moments in travel that strips everything back and reminds you what actually matters. We left with full hearts and a perspective on our own lives that we have never quite lost.

Eswatini is small. It is landlocked. It is not on most travel bucket lists. It absolutely should be.

Common questions

Eswatini FAQ

Is Eswatini worth visiting?
Absolutely — Eswatini is one of Africa's most overlooked destinations. It is a small landlocked kingdom with stunning landscapes, rich Swazi culture, welcoming people and a depth of cultural experience that is very hard to find elsewhere. If you are travelling through southern Africa it is absolutely worth making time for.
What is Eswatini like?
Eswatini — formerly known as Swaziland — is a small landlocked kingdom in southern Africa, surrounded by South Africa and Mozambique. It has beautiful highland and lowland landscapes, a proud and distinct Swazi cultural identity, and some of the most genuine and warm people we have encountered anywhere in Africa.
How do you get to Eswatini?
The easiest way to visit Eswatini is as part of a southern Africa trip — it is very accessible by road from South Africa and makes a brilliant stop between Johannesburg and the Kruger National Park or the Mozambique coast. We visited as part of a South Africa trip in 2018 and it was one of the highlights of the entire journey.
What is Mbabane like?
Mbabane is the capital of Eswatini — a small, characterful city set in the Mdimba Mountains with a relaxed atmosphere, good local markets and a genuine sense of Swazi culture and identity. It has a warmth and character that makes it a very pleasant base for exploring the kingdom.
What cultural experiences can you have in Eswatini?
Eswatini offers some of the most authentic cultural experiences in southern Africa — visits to traditional Swazi villages and homesteads, traditional crafts, music and dance, and the opportunity to spend time with local communities in a way that is respectful and genuinely enriching. We spent time with a local tribe and visited a primary school — both were among the most moving experiences of our time in Africa.
