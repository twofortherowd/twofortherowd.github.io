🇹🇿 The Trip

The honeymoon story

The most extraordinary start to a honeymoon we could have imagined.

"Zanzibar is one of those places that hits you the moment you arrive and never quite lets go. The smells, the sounds, the heat, the history — it is completely and utterly unlike anywhere else we have ever been."

We chose Zanzibar as the first stop on our 2023 honeymoon — and it set the bar extraordinarily high. Based in Stone Town for four nights, we were immediately immersed in one of the most atmospheric and historically rich places we have ever stayed. The old city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and every narrow lane tells a story — the Arabic architecture, the ornate carved wooden doors, the mosques and churches standing almost side by side, the scent of spices drifting through the air.

One of the things that struck us most was the remarkable harmony between Zanzibar's different religious communities. The island is predominantly Muslim, but Christianity is also very much present — and the two coexist with a warmth and mutual respect that is genuinely inspiring. It gave the whole place a feeling of peace and openness that we have rarely encountered.

The island tour was extraordinary — a full day taking in the diversity of Zanzibar's landscapes and communities, culminating in a swim in a cave that was one of those genuinely magical travel moments you never forget. Prison Island was a brilliant half day — the giant Aldabra tortoises alone are worth the boat trip — and having a local guide show us around Stone Town gave us a completely different perspective on the city.

A few practical notes for anyone planning a visit: bring plenty of cash — cards are not widely accepted and the markets and bazaars run entirely on haggling. And prepare yourself for the roads. The potholes in Zanzibar are legendary. Go with the flow, hold on tight and enjoy the ride.

Common questions

Zanzibar FAQ

Is Zanzibar worth visiting for a honeymoon?
Absolutely — Zanzibar is one of the world's great honeymoon destinations. The combination of stunning beaches, fascinating Stone Town, incredible snorkelling and diving, spice tours and the unique cultural mix of African, Arab and Indian influences makes it unlike anywhere else. It is romantic, exotic and genuinely unforgettable.
Is Stone Town worth staying in?
Yes — staying in Stone Town gives you a completely different experience to the beach resort areas of Zanzibar. The labyrinthine streets, the ornate carved doors, the call to prayer echoing across the rooftops, the mix of mosques and churches — it is one of the most atmospheric places we have ever stayed. Having a local guide show you around is highly recommended.
Is Prison Island worth visiting from Zanzibar?
Yes — Prison Island is a brilliant half day trip from Stone Town. The short boat ride over is lovely, the island has an interesting history as a former quarantine station, and the giant Aldabra tortoises that live there are an absolute highlight. Well worth including in your Zanzibar itinerary.
Should you bring cash to Zanzibar?
Yes — definitely bring cash to Zanzibar. While larger hotels and resorts accept cards, cash is king in the markets, smaller restaurants and for haggling in the Stone Town bazaars. US dollars are widely accepted alongside Tanzanian shillings. Bring more than you think you need — the haggling culture is wonderful but you will want cash to hand at all times.
What is the culture like in Zanzibar?
Zanzibar has one of the most fascinating cultural mixes anywhere in the world — a blend of African, Arab, Indian and European influences that reflects centuries of trade and history. The island is predominantly Muslim but has a significant Christian community, and the two coexist with a warmth and harmony that is genuinely inspiring. The people are incredibly welcoming and proud of their island's unique identity.
What are the roads like in Zanzibar?
Honest answer — the roads in Zanzibar are not great. Potholes are a fact of life, particularly outside the main tourist areas, and journeys can take longer than you expect. Go with it — it is all part of the adventure and the local drivers navigate it all with impressive skill and good humour!

What to pack for Zanzibar

Zanzibar packing essentials — what we'd bring

Zanzibar is hot and humid year-round — temperatures typically sit around 28–32°C. Pack light and breathable clothes, bring strong sun protection and remember that Stone Town is a predominantly Muslim area so modest dress is respectful when exploring the old city.
