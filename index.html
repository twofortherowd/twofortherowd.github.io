Common questions

Morocco tour FAQ

Is a Morocco tour actually worth it?
Yes — genuinely. The distances involved and the complexity of getting around Morocco make a guided tour much better value than trying to piece it together yourself. You see more, stress less, and get access to things you'd never find on your own. We'd do it again without hesitation.
Is TourRadar reliable for booking Morocco tours?
We booked ours through TourRadar and had zero issues. It's a reputable platform, well established, and the booking process was smooth. The key is choosing the right tour operator — we went with Morhara Morocco Tours and would recommend them.
How many days do you need in Morocco?
To do it properly — including Marrakech, the Atlas Mountains, and the Sahara — you need at least 7–10 days. Anything less and you're rushing.
Is Morocco good for gluten-free travellers?
Better than you'd expect. Tagines, grilled meats, rice dishes, and lots of naturally gluten-free Moroccan food make it manageable. Just be clear when ordering and you'll be fine. We didn't have any problems.
What's the Sahara desert camp actually like?
Genuinely one of the best nights of our lives. Watching the sun set over the dunes, having dinner under the stars, waking up for sunrise in the desert — it's one of those experiences that's hard to put into words.
