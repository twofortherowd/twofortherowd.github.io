About Us

Two for the Row’d is what happens when two Irish people who love a good story, a bit of craic, and the odd adventure decide to bring the internet with them.

We’re Nicola and Padraig — husband, wife, and travel partners in crime from Carlow — and we document the places we go, the things we see, the food we eat (especially the gluten-free gems), and the little moments that make travel memorable.

Not the polished postcard version.
The real one.

We share travel the way it actually happens: getting lost, finding hidden gems, chatting to locals, laughing at ourselves, learning bits of history along the way, and always coming home with a story to tell.

From heritage spots around Ireland to far-flung corners of the world, our content blends:

  • Storytelling
  • Humour
  • Local history
  • Practical tips for real travellers
  • A strong dose of Irish personality

Across Instagram, TikTok, Facebook and YouTube, our growing audience follows along because it feels like they’re travelling with us, not watching from a distance.

We’re proud TourRadar Ambassadors, partnering with one of the world’s leading adventure travel platforms to inspire people to see more of the world — one trip, one story, one reel at a time.

Morocco Series

Our Morocco Journey

Morocco had been on our travel list for a long time, and this adventure brought us through some of the most incredible landscapes and cultural experiences we’ve ever seen. From the vibrant streets of Marrakech to the vast silence of the Sahara Desert, this journey took us across mountains, deserts, ancient towns and traditional villages.

Our trip was booked through TourRadar and the tour itself was run by the fantastic team at Morhara Morocco Tours, who guided us through some of the most breathtaking parts of the country. We also explored the historic streets of Marrakech on a walking tour with Ali from Get Marrakech, which gave us a brilliant introduction to the city's culture, food and history.

Over the course of the journey we travelled through the dramatic peaks of the Atlas Mountains, visited traditional Berber towns, explored UNESCO heritage sites, and experienced the magic of the Sahara — including an unforgettable night in a desert camp under the stars.

Along the way we met local artisans, visited women’s cooperatives producing traditional Moroccan products, explored ancient caravan routes, and discovered hidden oases that felt like something from another world.

This Morocco series follows the full route of our journey — from city rooftops and bustling souks to remote desert dunes and mountain villages. Each stop along the way tells a different story about Morocco’s history, culture and landscapes.
