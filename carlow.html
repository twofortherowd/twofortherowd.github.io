🤯 Mad Facts About Carlow

The stories that make people say "wait, that happened in Carlow?"

A doctor who blew up a medieval castle trying to convert it into a lunatic asylum. A Gothic mansion burned to the ground under suspicious circumstances with a banshee curse attached. And a prehistoric tomb with a capstone heavier than anything else in Europe — sitting in a field three kilometres from the town centre. Carlow, ladies and gentlemen.

💣
The Doctor Who Blew Up Carlow Castle

In 1814, a local physician called Dr Philip Parry Middleton leased Carlow Castle — a magnificent 13th-century Norman fortress that had survived 600 years of war, sieges and Cromwell — with the intention of converting it into a lunatic asylum. To create more space, he decided to use gunpowder to thin the walls. He used rather too much. The two eastern towers and their adjoining walls collapsed completely. Today, only the western wall and two towers remain. One historian of the time described him as "a ninny-pated physician" who used "blasts of gunpowder for enlarging the windows and diminishing the walls." If it had survived intact, Carlow Castle would have been comparable in scale and grandeur to Kilkenny Castle.

🔥
Duckett's Grove — the Banshee, the Curse and the Mysterious Fire

About ten kilometres from Carlow town sit the extraordinary Gothic ruins of Duckett's Grove — once the centrepiece of a 12,000-acre estate. On the night of 20 April 1933, the house burned to the ground. The cause was never officially established. A legend holds that a young woman had an affair with one of the Ducketts, died after a fall from her horse, and her mother placed a Piseóg on the family. A banshee is said to haunt the ruined towers to this day. The ruins and walled gardens are free to visit.

🪨
Browne's Hill Dolmen — Older Than the Pyramids, Three Minutes from Town

Three kilometres east of Carlow town sits one of the most extraordinary prehistoric monuments in Europe. Browne's Hill Dolmen is a Neolithic portal tomb built between 4,000 and 3,300 BC — predating the Egyptian pyramids by over a thousand years. Its granite capstone weighs an estimated 100 to 150 tonnes, making it the heaviest megalithic capstone in Europe. The site has never been excavated. Free to visit, open always.

❓ Carlow FAQ

Common questions about County Carlow

What is Carlow famous for?
Carlow is famous for its extraordinary history — Walt Disney's ancestors are buried here, Myles Keogh who died at Custer's Last Stand was from Leighlinbridge, 22 early Christian saints came from one small area of the county, and Browne's Hill Dolmen just outside town has the largest megalithic capstone in Europe. Oh, and a local doctor blew up Carlow Castle in 1814 trying to convert it into a lunatic asylum.
Is Carlow worth visiting?
Absolutely. Carlow is one of the most underrated counties in Ireland — small, compact and packed with extraordinary history. Browne's Hill Dolmen, Carlow Castle, Altamont Gardens, Duckett's Grove, the County Museum and the Cathedral are all within easy reach of the town centre. An excellent day trip from Dublin or Kilkenny.
Did Walt Disney have Irish roots in Carlow?
Yes — Walt Disney's ancestors on his father's side were from County Carlow, and members of the Disney family are buried in the county. One of the most extraordinary and least-known historical connections in Ireland.
Who was Myles Keogh?
Myles Keogh was born in Leighlinbridge, County Carlow. He served in the Papal Army in Rome, fought in the American Civil War and was killed at the Battle of the Little Bighorn — Custer's Last Stand — in 1876. His horse Comanche was the only US cavalry survivor of the battle.
What happened to Carlow Castle?
Carlow Castle survived 600 years of war and Cromwell — only to be largely destroyed in 1814 by Dr Philip Parry Middleton who leased it to convert into a lunatic asylum. He used gunpowder to thin the walls and used far too much, collapsing the two eastern towers. One historian called him "a ninny-pated physician."
What is Browne's Hill Dolmen?
Browne's Hill Dolmen is a Neolithic portal tomb built between 4,000 and 3,300 BC, located just 3km east of Carlow town. Its granite capstone weighs an estimated 100 to 150 tonnes — the heaviest megalithic capstone in Europe. It predates the Egyptian pyramids by over a thousand years, has never been excavated and is free to visit any time.
What is Duckett's Grove?
Duckett's Grove is a spectacular Gothic Revival mansion ruin about 10km from Carlow town. Once the centrepiece of a 12,000-acre estate, it burned to the ground in 1933 under suspicious circumstances. A banshee curse legend is attached to the ruins. The walled gardens are open to the public for free.
