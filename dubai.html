🇦🇪 The Story

Dubai — two nights that packed in more than you'd expect

"The heat when we stepped off the plane in the evening was something else. A wall of warm air that hits you immediately and reminds you very firmly that you are no longer in Ireland. Or South Africa. Or anywhere you've been before."

Dubai was a stopover on the way home from South Africa in 2019 — two nights on the Marina, squeezed in between one adventure and the flight home. It turned out to be a brilliant decision. Even 48 hours in Dubai gives you enough to understand why the city has become one of the most visited destinations on earth.

The Marina was the perfect base — beautifully designed, walkable along the waterfront, with the extraordinary skyline rising on all sides. Staying there gives you an immediate sense of the ambition and scale of what Dubai has built in an extraordinarily short space of time. The Burj Khalifa dominates everything — even from the Marina it is an unmistakable presence on the horizon.

Day one was the city tour. Day two was the pool. The heat on day two was genuinely ferocious — the kind of heat that makes you very happy there is a pool and a sun lounger and coconut water on tap. And then the evening saved everything — ladies night, half price cocktails and a Dubai night out that was brilliant craic. Expensive city, but that one was good value. 😄

🌆 Dubai Marina

The Marina — the best place to base yourself in Dubai

The Dubai Marina is one of the most impressive urban developments in the world — a purpose-built waterfront district of skyscrapers, hotels, restaurants and a beautiful marina walk that stretches along the water. Staying here puts you right in the heart of modern Dubai, with everything within walking distance and the famous JBR beach just minutes away.

The skyline from the Marina at night is extraordinary — towers lit up in every direction, the reflections in the water, the boats and yachts moored along the waterfront. It is one of those cityscapes that you genuinely have to see in person to appreciate the scale of it.

🌡️ About the heat: Stepping off the plane in Dubai in the evening — when it has supposedly "cooled down" — is a physical shock if you are not prepared for it. The air is warm, heavy and immediately enveloping. If you are visiting in summer, factor in that you will be spending significant time indoors or in the pool. This is not a complaint. The pools are excellent.
🏙️ The City Tour

What to see in Dubai — the highlights

🏗️
Burj Khalifa

The tallest building in the world at 828 metres — visible from everywhere in the city and genuinely awe-inspiring even from the ground. We didn't go up on this visit but the views from the base are extraordinary in themselves. If you have time, booking the observation deck is absolutely worth it.

🛍️
The Malls

Dubai Mall — attached to the Burj Khalifa — is one of the largest shopping malls in the world and contains an indoor ice rink, an aquarium and more shops than you could visit in a week. We were tempted but the heat outside made the pool win on day two. Absolutely worth a visit if conditions allow.

🕌
Old Dubai — the Creek & Souks

The older part of Dubai around the Creek — the Gold Souk, the Spice Souk, the traditional abra water taxis crossing the water — offers a completely different side of the city to the Marina and Downtown. Fascinating and well worth including on any city tour.

🏜️
Desert Safari

One of the most popular Dubai experiences — a desert safari taking you out into the dunes for sandboarding, camel riding, a traditional Bedouin camp dinner and stargazing. We didn't do it on this visit but it is absolutely on the list for next time.

🌊
JBR Beach

Jumeirah Beach Residence — the beach right beside the Marina — is one of Dubai's most popular and accessible beaches. Clean, well-maintained and with the extraordinary backdrop of the Marina towers behind you.

🏛️
Museum of the Future

One of the most architecturally stunning buildings in the world — a torus-shaped structure covered in Arabic calligraphy that houses exhibitions on future technology and innovation. An extraordinary addition to the Dubai skyline.

🍹 Ladies Night

Ladies night — Dubai's best kept value secret

Dubai is an expensive city — there is no getting around it. Alcohol in particular is heavily taxed and a round of drinks can come as a genuine shock. Which is why ladies night is such a brilliant institution. Most bars and restaurants across the Marina and Downtown offer women heavily discounted or complimentary cocktails on specific nights of the week.

We found ours, got our half price cocktails, had a brilliant evening and felt considerably better about Dubai's price point as a result. If you are visiting Dubai and you are a woman or with women — find out which night is ladies night in your area of the city and plan around it. It makes a significant difference to the bill.

🍸 Ladies night tip: Ladies night nights vary by venue and day of the week — Tuesday and Wednesday are common but check in advance. Most venues in the Marina, JBR and Downtown areas participate. Google "ladies night Dubai [current week]" for the latest deals.
💬 Honest Verdict

Our honest take on Dubai

"Dubai is expensive, the heat is ferocious and the scale of everything is almost overwhelming. But it is also extraordinary — a city unlike any other on earth, with a skyline that genuinely takes your breath away and a energy that is completely unique. Two nights was enough to get a real taste of it. We'd go back."

Dubai divides opinion — some people love the ambition and glamour of it, others find the artificiality jarring. We found ourselves somewhere in between. The skyline is genuinely extraordinary. The Marina is a great base. The people — a mix of Emiratis, expats from all over the world and tourists — are warm and friendly. The heat in summer is a genuine consideration.

The expense is real. Dubai is not a cheap city and if you want to do it properly — good hotel, meals out, drinks, experiences — the costs add up quickly. Budget carefully. Find the ladies nights. And book the Burj Khalifa observation deck in advance if you can — we regret not going up.

Would we go back? Yes — there is more to see. The desert safari, Abu Dhabi and the Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque, the Burj Khalifa from the top. Dubai rewards more than two nights if you can give it.

Common questions

Dubai FAQ

Is Dubai worth visiting for a short stopover?
Yes — even two nights gives you a real taste of Dubai. The Marina is a brilliant base, the city tour covers the main highlights and the contrast between the ultramodern skyline and the older creek and souk areas is genuinely fascinating. Just be prepared for the heat if you are visiting in summer.
How hot is Dubai?
Very. Dubai in summer can reach 40-45°C with high humidity — stepping off the plane in the evening and being hit by a wall of heat is a genuine shock to the system. If you are sensitive to heat, visit between November and March when temperatures are much more manageable at around 20-25°C.
What is ladies night in Dubai?
Ladies night is a Dubai institution — many bars and restaurants offer women free drinks or heavily discounted cocktails on specific nights of the week, usually Tuesday or Wednesday. It is brilliant value in a city that can otherwise be very expensive for drinking out. Check which venues near you are participating before you go.
Is Dubai expensive?
Yes — Dubai is one of the more expensive cities in the world, particularly for food, drink and accommodation. Alcohol is heavily taxed and a round of drinks in a bar can come as a shock. Ladies night is one of the best ways to enjoy Dubai's nightlife without completely emptying your wallet. Budget carefully and look for deals.
Is the Dubai Marina a good area to stay?
Yes — the Marina is one of the best areas to base yourself in Dubai. It is modern, beautifully designed, walkable along the waterfront and has a great range of restaurants and bars. The famous JBR beach is nearby and the views of the Marina skyline are stunning, particularly at night.
