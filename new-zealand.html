The trip

Both islands — and every day felt different

We travelled across both islands — from the dramatic peaks of the South Island to the volcanic landscapes and Maori culture of the North. The variety is extraordinary. No two days on this trip looked anything alike.

The South Island gave us Queenstown — the adventure capital of the world — Milford Sound, the Franz Josef glacier and some of the most dramatic mountain and lake scenery we've ever encountered anywhere in 57 countries between us.

The North Island brought a completely different energy — the geothermal weirdness of Rotorua, the magic of the glowworm caves at Waitomo, the unmissable pilgrimage to Hobbiton, jet boating on the Taupo rapids, and the stunning Bay of Islands to finish.

If Australia was the trip of a lifetime, New Zealand was the one that made us realise the world is even more extraordinary than we thought. In 57 countries, it still stands apart.

The highlights

The places that stopped us in our tracks

🌄
Milford Sound

The most dramatic fjord in New Zealand — sheer cliff faces, waterfalls dropping hundreds of metres and an atmosphere that feels completely otherworldly.

✨
Glowworm Caves

The Waitomo glowworm caves are one of the most magical experiences in New Zealand. Floating through a cave lit entirely by thousands of tiny glowworms is genuinely unforgettable.

🪂
Queenstown

The adventure capital of the world — and it earns the title. Bungee jumping, skydiving, jet boating, stunning lake views. One of the most energetic places we've ever been.

🧊
Franz Josef Glacier

Walking on a glacier that has been forming for thousands of years — in the middle of a lush rainforest setting — is one of those experiences that puts everything in perspective.

🧙
Hobbiton

Even if you're not a Lord of the Rings superfan, Hobbiton is a genuinely magical experience. The attention to detail is extraordinary and the setting is beautiful.

🐋
Kaikoura Wildlife

Whale watching, dolphins and seals all in one stop. Kaikoura is one of the best places in the world for marine wildlife — it completely delivers.

🚤 Taupo Rapids Jet

One of the most fun things we did

The Taupo Rapids Jet — absolutely mad craic

If you want pure adrenaline without jumping out of a plane, the Taupo Rapids jet boat is it. Screaming through narrow river canyons at full speed, spinning 360s on the water — it is one of those experiences where you spend the whole time laughing and screaming simultaneously. Common questions

New Zealand FAQ

Is New Zealand worth visiting?
Absolutely — New Zealand is one of the most visually extraordinary places on earth. The scenery genuinely does not look real in places. From the fjords of Milford Sound to the geothermal landscape of Rotorua, the adventure capital of Queenstown and Hobbiton, it is one of those trips that stays with you forever. In 57 countries between us, New Zealand still stands apart.
What are the best things to do in New Zealand?
The highlights include Milford Sound, the glowworm caves at Waitomo, adventure activities in Queenstown, the Franz Josef glacier, Hobbiton, the geothermal landscape of Rotorua, the Taupo rapids jet boat and the Bay of Islands. Every single stop feels completely different — that variety is one of the best things about New Zealand.
Is a guided tour of New Zealand worth it?
Yes — New Zealand is large and the highlights are spread across both islands. A guided tour means you see far more without the stress of organising transport, ferries and accommodation between every stop. It is the most efficient and enjoyable way to experience both the North and South Islands properly.
How long do you need in New Zealand?
To do both islands justice — South Island highlights like Milford Sound and Queenstown plus North Island stops like Rotorua, Hobbiton and the Bay of Islands — allow at least two to three weeks. Rushing New Zealand would be a real mistake. The drives between stops are part of the experience.
Can you combine Australia and New Zealand on one trip?
Yes — and it makes a brilliant combined adventure. We flew from Australia into Christchurch and did New Zealand as the second part of the same trip. The two countries complement each other perfectly — Australia gives you the cities, beaches and Outback, New Zealand gives you the dramatic scenery and pure adventure. The flights between them are short and straightforward.
