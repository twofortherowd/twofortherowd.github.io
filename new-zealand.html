🇳🇿 The Story

19 days. Two islands. A trip we'll never forget.

"New Zealand is in our top five countries in the world. The scenery, the people, the craic — the bars, the atmosphere. The Kiwis are some of the nicest people we have ever met anywhere. If you're even half-thinking about going, just go. You won't regret a single second of it."

We flew into Auckland from Hobart having already spent time in Perth and Tasmania as part of a longer Australia and New Zealand adventure. By the time we landed in Auckland we were well-travelled — but New Zealand still managed to completely blow us away from day one.

The Haka Tours Amplified 19-day tour was the perfect way to see both islands. A small group, a brilliant guide, everything organised — all we had to do was show up and enjoy it. And enjoy it we absolutely did. White water rafting, glowworm caves, Hobbiton, a Māori hāngī and haka that genuinely moved us, stargazing at 1,029 metres above Lake Tekapo in the freezing cold with a hot chocolate in hand — and finishing the tour on a birthday in Christchurch with an eye infection from sheer exhaustion. Every single bit of it was worth it. 😄

🌿 North Island Highlights

Auckland & Coromandel — the beautiful start

We had one night in Auckland — just enough to get our bearings in New Zealand's largest city before the tour began. Auckland sits on a narrow isthmus between two harbours and the skyline, dominated by the Sky Tower, is genuinely impressive.

Coromandel was the first real wow moment of the tour. The drive up the peninsula is stunning but it's Cathedral Cove that stops you in your tracks — a magnificent natural rock arch opening onto a pristine beach that looks like it belongs on a screensaver. And in fact, it has been a Google screensaver! There's a bit of a hike down to reach it but every step is worth it. One of the most beautiful spots in New Zealand.

✨ Waitomo Glowworm Caves — pure magic

The Waitomo glowworm caves are one of the most magical experiences in New Zealand — and that is not an exaggeration. Floating through a vast underground cave in total silence, looking up at a ceiling covered in thousands of tiny glowworms creating their own galaxy above you — it is genuinely one of the most extraordinary natural experiences we have ever had.

The silence, the darkness and the sheer otherworldliness of it makes Waitomo completely unlike anything else on the tour. Don't skip it. If you want the full adrenaline version, black water rafting takes you through the cave on an inflated tube — thrilling, wet and brilliantly mad craic.

🧙 Hobbiton — more magical than you expect

Even if you've never watched a single Lord of the Rings film, Hobbiton is worth doing. The attention to detail is extraordinary — every hobbit hole, every garden, every detail of Bag End is maintained exactly as it appeared on screen. The Waikato hills rolling away in every direction provide a backdrop that feels genuinely Middle-earth.

The guided tour brings it all to life in a way that just wandering around independently never could. And finishing with a drink at the Green Dragon Inn is a lovely touch. One of New Zealand's most iconic experiences — and it fully earns that status.

🎭 The Māori Cultural Night

The Māori hāngī and haka — genuinely one of the most moving experiences of the trip

"The haka was extraordinary. You know it's coming, you've seen it a thousand times on TV — and it still absolutely floored us. The power of it, the emotion, the connection to something ancient and proud. We were genuinely moved."

The Māori cultural evening was one of the standout experiences of the entire 19 days — and neither of us had quite anticipated how much it would affect us. The evening included a traditional hāngī meal — food slow-cooked in an underground pit using heated rocks, the traditional Māori method of cooking for centuries. The result is smoky, tender and completely unlike anything you've had before.

But the haka performance was what stayed with us. Watching and participating in a proper haka — the stomping, the chanting, the intensity of eye contact and the sheer physical power of the performance — is something that gets into you in a way that is very hard to explain. It is not entertainment. It is something much older and more profound than that.

Learning about Māori culture, history and traditions from the people themselves gave the whole evening a depth and authenticity that made it one of the most memorable nights of our entire time in New Zealand. Absolutely do not skip this if you go.

🌌 Lake Tekapo — Stargazing on Mount John

Stargazing on Mount John — the southern lights in the freezing cold

Lake Tekapo is already one of the most beautiful places in New Zealand — the extraordinary turquoise glacial water, the Church of the Good Shepherd on the shore, the mountains rising on all sides. But it is what happens after dark that makes it truly extraordinary.

The Mount John Observatory sits 1,029 metres above the lake within the world's largest International Dark Sky Reserve — one of the darkest, clearest skies anywhere on the planet. We went up to the summit for the stargazing experience and the southern skies were breathtaking. The Milky Way stretching overhead, the southern constellations, and — on our night — the Southern Lights dancing across the sky.

It was absolutely freezing cold at the summit — genuine polar jacket territory — but nobody cared because what was happening above us was too extraordinary to feel anything but awe. Hot chocolate in hand, slightly too many drinks taken earlier in the evening, standing on top of a mountain looking at the aurora australis. One of those moments that doesn't feel real while it's happening.

☕ Pro tip from bitter experience: Wrap up like you're going to Antarctica. It is COLD on that summit. The hot chocolate is not optional.

🪂 South Island Adventures

Queenstown, Wanaka, Milford Sound & Franz Josef

Queenstown is the adventure capital of the world and it absolutely earns the title. The setting alone — on the shores of Lake Wakatipu with the Remarkables mountain range rising dramatically behind — is extraordinary. Add bungee jumping, skydiving, jet boating and white water rafting and you have a place that is endlessly, gloriously energetic.

Wanaka sits just over the Crown Range from Queenstown and offers the same stunning lake and mountain scenery with a fraction of the crowds. The famous Wanaka tree in the lake has been photographed millions of times for good reason — it is one of those perfectly composed natural scenes.

Milford Sound is the most dramatic fjord in New Zealand — sheer cliff faces dropping hundreds of metres into the dark water, waterfalls cascading from the peaks above, and an atmosphere that feels completely otherworldly even when it's raining. Especially when it's raining, actually. The boat cruise through the sound is one of the great New Zealand experiences.

Franz Josef Glacier sits surrounded by lush rainforest in a setting that makes no sense until you're standing in it. Walking up to the glacier face with the jungle all around you is one of those genuinely surreal New Zealand moments — the kind that makes you understand why people say the country doesn't look real.

🚤 Taupō Rapids Jet

The Taupō Rapids Jet — absolutely mad craic

If you want pure adrenaline without jumping out of a plane, the Taupō Rapids jet boat is it. Screaming through narrow river canyons at full speed, spinning 360s on the water — it is one of those experiences where you spend the whole time laughing and screaming simultaneously. 🎂 Christchurch — The End of an Epic Adventure

Finishing the tour in Christchurch — on a birthday, with an eye infection

"By the time we reached Christchurch we had been travelling from Perth to Hobart to Auckland and all the way through New Zealand. My body simply said enough. The eye infection was my immune system's way of telling me to go home and sleep for a week."

The tour finished in Christchurch — a city still rebuilding its identity and its physical fabric after the devastating earthquakes of 2010 and 2011, but doing so with a remarkable spirit and creativity. The container malls, the street art, the rebuilt cathedral precinct — Christchurch has something genuinely moving about it.

We celebrated the birthday here — or tried to. Between the exhaustion of 19 days of adventuring on top of weeks already spent in Australia, the immune system had simply had enough. An eye infection meant a trip to the doctor rather than the bar. The doctor was very nice. The eye recovered. The birthday was salvaged. 😄

But even that ending couldn't diminish what the 19 days had been. New Zealand is one of those places that gets into your soul in a way that very few destinations manage. The people, the landscapes, the culture, the craic — it is extraordinary from start to finish.

Common questions

New Zealand FAQ

Is New Zealand worth visiting?
Absolutely — New Zealand is firmly in our top five countries in the world and we'd go back tomorrow. The scenery genuinely does not look real in places, the people are some of the nicest we've encountered anywhere, and the variety of experiences — from Māori culture to glaciers to glowworm caves to the best adventure sports on earth — is extraordinary.
What is a Māori hāngī?
A hāngī is a traditional Māori method of cooking — food is slow-cooked in an underground pit using heated rocks. The result is smoky, tender and completely unlike anything you've had before. Experiencing a hāngī meal as part of a Māori cultural evening — with a haka performance and traditional songs — is one of the most moving and memorable experiences available in New Zealand. Do not skip it.
Is stargazing at Lake Tekapo worth it?
Yes — Lake Tekapo is one of the best places on earth to stargaze. The Mount John Observatory sits 1,029 metres above the lake within the world's largest International Dark Sky Reserve. On a clear night the Southern Lights, the Milky Way and the southern sky are genuinely breathtaking. Wrap up very warm — it gets extremely cold on the summit. The hot chocolate is not optional.
Is Hobbiton worth visiting?
Yes — even if you're not a Lord of the Rings fan. The attention to detail is extraordinary, the setting in the Waikato hills is beautiful and the fully guided tour brings it all to life. One of New Zealand's most iconic experiences and it fully earns that status.
Is a guided tour the best way to see New Zealand?
For first-time visitors, yes — New Zealand is large and the highlights are spread across both islands. A guided tour like the Haka Tours Amplified means you cover far more ground without the stress of organising transport, ferries and accommodation yourself. It is the most efficient and enjoyable way to experience both islands properly in a limited time.
How long do you need in New Zealand?
To do both islands justice allow at least two to three weeks. We did 19 days on the Haka Tours Amplified and felt we got a brilliant but still fast-moving overview of both islands. If you can give it longer, do. Rushing New Zealand would be a real mistake — the drives between stops are part of the experience.
