🇧🇪 The Story

A year that changed everything

From study abroad to a love affair with Belgium that never ended.

"Brussels didn't just grow on me — it got completely under my skin. There is something about the city that is impossible to fully explain until you have lived it. The culture, the food, the beer, the people, the pace of life. I loved every single day of it." — Nicola

Nicola arrived in Brussels in 2005 to study and ended up staying a full year — one of the best years of her life. Brussels is a city that rewards time. On the surface it can seem a little grey, a little bureaucratic — it is the capital of Europe, after all — but scratch beneath that surface and you find a city that is vibrant, warm, deeply cultured and endlessly interesting.

The food and drink culture alone would be enough to keep you entertained for years. Belgium has a genuine claim to producing the best beer in the world — and the tradition runs deep. The famous saying goes that there are 365 beers brewed in Brussels — one for every day of the year — and sampling your way through them in the city's incredible bars and cafés is one of life's great pleasures.

The waffles. The chocolate. The frites with proper Belgian mayonnaise. The mussels. The stoemp. The cuisine of Belgium is extraordinary and criminally underappreciated on the world stage — every meal is an event.

And then there is Grand Place — one of the most breathtaking squares anywhere in Europe. A UNESCO World Heritage Site ringed by ornate Gothic and Baroque guild houses and the soaring Town Hall. No matter how many times you cross it, it stops you in your tracks.

But what made the year truly special was the freedom Brussels gave to explore the rest of this extraordinary little country. Belgium is small but packed with incredible places — Ghent and Bruges are among the most beautiful medieval cities in Europe, Liège has a brilliant gritty charm, and getting to the Spa-Francorchamps F1 circuit for a race was one of those bucket-list moments that delivered everything it promised and more.

The highlights — what Brussels does best

🏛️
Grand Place

One of Europe's most spectacular squares — a UNESCO World Heritage Site surrounded by ornate guild houses and the magnificent Town Hall. Breathtaking day and night.

🍺
Belgian Beer Culture

365 beers brewed in Brussels alone — one for every day of the year. Belgium's beer tradition is recognised by UNESCO and the bars and cafés to enjoy it in are world class.

⚛️
The Atomium

Brussels' most iconic and unusual landmark — a giant model of an iron crystal built for the 1958 World Expo. Quirky, futuristic and completely unlike anything else in Europe.

🧇
Waffles & Chocolate

Belgian waffles and chocolate are not a cliché — they are genuinely, seriously, world-stoppingly good. Every visit to a waffle stand or chocolatier is a highlight in itself.

🏁
Spa-Francorchamps F1

Getting to the iconic Belgian Grand Prix circuit at Spa for a race is a bucket-list experience. The track, the atmosphere and the Ardennes setting make it one of the best sporting events in the world.

🤝
The People

Belgians get unfairly overlooked in the European friendliness rankings — they are warm, funny, proud of their country and genuinely welcoming to visitors who take the time to engage.

🚂 Beyond Brussels

Day trips across Belgium — a country full of gems

One of the great things about Brussels is how brilliantly placed it is to explore the rest of Belgium. Nicola made the most of this during her year — travelling from north to south, east to west, and finding something wonderful around every corner.

🏰
Bruges

One of the most perfectly preserved medieval cities in the world. Canals, chocolate shops and cobbled streets — under an hour from Brussels by train.

🏙️
Ghent

Bruges' slightly grittier, younger sibling — vibrant, artistic and full of brilliant bars and restaurants. A favourite of those who know Belgium well.

🌊
Liège

A characterful city in the east with a strong sense of Walloon identity, a brilliant street market and a warm, slightly rougher-around-the-edges charm.

🏁
Spa

Famous for its mineral springs — and for Spa-Francorchamps, one of the greatest Formula 1 circuits in the world, set in the beautiful Ardennes hills.

Common questions

Brussels & Belgium FAQ

Is Brussels worth visiting?
Absolutely — Brussels is one of Europe's most underrated city break destinations. It has world-class architecture, incredible food and drink, fascinating history and a wonderfully relaxed atmosphere. Grand Place alone is worth the trip, but there is so much more beyond it.
How many beers are brewed in Belgium?
Belgium has an extraordinary brewing tradition with hundreds of distinct beers — the saying goes there are 365 beers brewed in Brussels alone, one for every day of the year. Belgian beer culture is recognised by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.
What day trips can you do from Brussels?
Brussels is brilliantly placed for day trips across Belgium. Ghent and Bruges are both under an hour by train and are among the most beautiful medieval cities in Europe. Liège is a lively and characterful city further east, and Spa — famous for its F1 circuit and mineral springs — is also easily reachable.
What is Grand Place like?
Grand Place is one of the most spectacular squares in all of Europe — a UNESCO World Heritage Site surrounded by ornate Gothic and Baroque guild houses and the magnificent Town Hall. It is breathtaking at any time of day but particularly magical when illuminated at night.
What is the best time of year to visit Brussels?
Brussels is a great city year-round. Spring and early summer bring lovely weather for café terraces and exploring on foot. Christmas in Brussels is magical with stunning markets. Summer is lively and warm. Whenever you go, Brussels rewards the visit.
Is Brussels easy to get to from Ireland?
Yes — Brussels is very well connected from Ireland with direct flights from Dublin and Belfast. Flight time is around 1 hour 40 minutes. It is one of the most accessible European city break destinations for Irish travellers.
