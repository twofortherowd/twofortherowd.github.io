🇲🇨 The Story

The world's most glamorous square kilometre

A principality that earns every bit of its legendary reputation.

"You step off the train at Monaco-Monte-Carlo station and within about thirty seconds you see your first Lamborghini, your first superyacht and your first person dressed better than you will ever dress. And somehow it doesn't feel intimidating — it just feels magnificent."

We've done Monaco as a day trip from Nice several times now and we never tire of it. The train journey alone is worth it — the line hugs the Mediterranean coastline from Nice, passing through tunnels and clifftop villages with views that make you put your phone away and just look. Twenty-five minutes later you're in another world entirely.

Monte Carlo is the heart of it all — the harbour packed with some of the most extraordinary yachts you will ever see, the Casino square with its famous fountain and its parade of supercars, and the Casino de Monte-Carlo itself, which is even more beautiful in person than in any photograph. The Belle Époque architecture, the manicured gardens, the sheer extravagance of it — it is one of the great buildings of Europe.

Up on Le Rocher — the Rock — you find the older, quieter Monaco. The Prince's Palace where the Grimaldi family has lived for over 700 years, the Monaco Cathedral where Princess Grace and Prince Rainier are buried, the Justice Palace and the extraordinary Oceanographic Museum founded by Prince Albert I, which sits dramatically on the edge of the cliff above the Mediterranean.

The Princess Grace exhibition was one of the unexpected highlights of our visits. Grace Kelly was one of the great figures of the 20th century — Hollywood star, Princess of Monaco, style icon — and the exhibition does a beautiful job of telling her story. We came out genuinely moved. If you are visiting the Palace complex, do not skip it.

And then there is the matter of the cocktail. You owe it to yourself to sit at a harbour bar, order something cold and extravagantly priced, and watch the superyachts go by for an hour. It is expensive. It is completely worth it. It is Monaco.

The highlights — what Monaco does that nowhere else can

🎰
Casino de Monte-Carlo

One of the most beautiful and iconic buildings in Europe — the Belle Époque exterior alone is worth the visit. You can pay to enter the gaming rooms or simply admire the square, the fountain and the parade of supercars outside.

🏰
The Prince's Palace

The Grimaldi family residence for over 700 years — perched high above the harbour with incredible views over the principality. The State Apartments are open to visitors in summer and the daily Changing of the Guard at 11:55am is worth catching.

👸
Princess Grace Exhibition

An unexpectedly moving and beautifully curated exhibition about Grace Kelly — Hollywood actress, Princess of Monaco and one of the great style icons of the 20th century. Don't skip this one. We came out genuinely touched by it.

🐠
Oceanographic Museum

Founded by the scientist Prince Albert I — a genuinely world-class museum of marine science with a stunning aquarium, dramatic clifftop setting and incredible Mediterranean views from the terrace. One of Monaco's must-visits.

⛪
Monaco Cathedral

The Cathédrale de Monaco is a beautiful Romanesque-Byzantine building where both Princess Grace and Prince Rainier III are buried. Quiet, dignified and genuinely moving — a very different side of Monaco from the glamour outside.

🛥️
The Harbour & Yachts

Port Hercule is one of the great spectacles of the Mediterranean — superyachts that cost more than most towns, bobbing alongside each other in a harbour that has been dazzling visitors for centuries. Have that cocktail. You're in Monaco.

Common questions

Monaco FAQ

Is Monaco worth visiting as a day trip?
Absolutely — Monaco is one of those places that genuinely lives up to its reputation. The glamour, the superyachts, the Casino, the Palace and the extraordinary setting on the Côte d'Azur make it a brilliant day trip. It is small enough to cover on foot in a day but rich enough in things to see that you won't run out of things to do.
How do you get to Monaco from Nice?
The train from Nice to Monaco is one of the great short train journeys in Europe — it hugs the coastline and takes around 25 minutes. Trains run very frequently and it is by far the easiest and most enjoyable way to get there. No need to hire a car or take a taxi.
What is the Casino de Monte-Carlo like?
The Casino de Monte-Carlo is one of the most iconic and beautiful buildings in Europe — a stunning Belle Époque masterpiece set above the harbour with a square full of supercars outside. You can visit the casino floors (there is an entry fee) or simply admire the exterior and the atmosphere for free.
What is the Prince's Palace of Monaco like?
The Prince's Palace sits on the Rock of Monaco — Le Rocher — high above the harbour and offers stunning views over the principality and the Mediterranean. Parts of the palace are open to visitors and the State Apartments are impressive. The Changing of the Guard takes place daily at 11:55am.
Is the Oceanographic Museum Monaco worth visiting?
Yes — the Oceanographic Museum is one of Monaco's genuinely unmissable attractions. Founded by Prince Albert I, it sits dramatically on the edge of the Rock with incredible Mediterranean views and houses an impressive aquarium and fascinating marine science collections.
Is Monaco expensive?
Yes — Monaco is one of the most expensive places in the world. Cocktails, food and anything else you consume will cost significantly more than nearby Nice or the rest of the Côte d'Azur. That said, walking around, enjoying the views and seeing the sights is largely free. Budget for a drink or two by the harbour — it's worth the experience.
