🇲🇽 The Trip

Ten nights on the Riviera Maya

Sun, ancient wonders, a catamaran and the most entertaining hotel we've ever stayed in.

"The Barceló Maya Riviera set the bar so high for all-inclusive resort holidays that we're still talking about it. The pools, the beach, the food, the entertainment — and somewhere in the middle of it all, a bowling alley. A bowling alley. In a beach resort. We loved every single minute."

We arrived in Cancún in 2022 ready for a proper holiday — and the Barceló Maya Riviera delivered everything we were hoping for and more. This is a vast, beautiful all-inclusive resort complex on the Riviera Maya with multiple pools, a long stretch of private Caribbean beach, a huge choice of restaurants and bars, evening entertainment every night and a set of facilities that genuinely impressed us at every turn. The bowling alley in particular became something of a running joke — but we used it more than once.

The day trip to Chichén Itzá was one of the highlights of the entire trip. Seeing El Castillo — the great Mayan pyramid — in person is one of those genuinely humbling travel experiences. The scale of it, the history behind it, the precision of its construction — it is one of the Seven Wonders of the Modern World for very good reason. Book a guided tour to get the full story of what you're looking at.

The catamaran trip to Isla Mujeres was brilliant from start to finish — a beautiful small island just off the coast of Cancún with incredible turquoise water, great snorkelling, white sand beaches and a completely different pace of life to the hotel zone. One of those days where everything just works perfectly.

And then there was Coco Bongo. If you've been, you know. If you haven't — it is part nightclub, part Las Vegas spectacular, part fever dream, and entirely unlike anything else. Acrobats swinging from the rafters, tribute acts, confetti raining down, the whole crowd going absolutely mad. We had one night there and it was an experience we will never forget. Loud, spectacular and absolutely brilliant fun.

Common questions

Cancún & Mexico FAQ

Is Cancún worth visiting?
Absolutely — Cancún and the Riviera Maya offer some of the best all-inclusive resort experiences in the world, combined with genuinely incredible day trip options. Chichén Itzá, Isla Mujeres, cenotes, Tulum — the surrounding region is extraordinary. Cancún is the perfect base for it all.
What is the Barceló Maya Riviera like?
The Barceló Maya Riviera is a spectacular all-inclusive resort complex on the Riviera Maya with multiple pools, a private beach, a huge range of restaurants and bars, entertainment every evening and facilities that include — remarkably — a bowling alley. It is pure luxury and genuinely one of the best resort stays we have ever had.
Is Chichén Itzá worth visiting from Cancún?
Yes — absolutely. Chichén Itzá is one of the Seven Wonders of the Modern World and seeing El Castillo pyramid in person is genuinely breathtaking. The drive from Cancún takes around 2.5 hours each way — book a guided day trip to make the most of it and to get the historical context that brings the whole site to life.
What is the Isla Mujeres catamaran trip like?
The catamaran trip to Isla Mujeres is one of the best day trips you can do from Cancún — a beautiful small island in the Caribbean with turquoise water, great snorkelling, lovely beaches and a relaxed atmosphere completely different to the hotel zone. The catamaran journey itself is great fun.
What is Coco Bongo like?
Coco Bongo is one of Cancún's most famous entertainment venues — part nightclub, part Las Vegas show, with live performers, acrobats, tribute acts, confetti cannons and an energy unlike anything else. It is loud, spectacular and brilliant fun. Not for everyone — but if you like a big night out it is an experience you won't forget.
What is the best time to visit Cancún?
The best time to visit Cancún is between December and April — the dry season, when temperatures are warm and sunny without being oppressively hot and humidity is lower. We went in 2022 and the weather was spectacular. The peak hurricane season runs from June to November so it's worth bearing that in mind when booking.
